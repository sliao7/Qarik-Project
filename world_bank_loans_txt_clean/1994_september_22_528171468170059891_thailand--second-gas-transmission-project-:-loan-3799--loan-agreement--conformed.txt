WEEREAS: (A) Kingdom of Thailand (the Guarantor) and the 
Borrower, having been satisfied as to the feasibility and priority 

of the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement, have 

requested the Bank to assist in the financing of the Project;   
(B) the Borrower has contracted from the Asian Development 

Bank (ADB) a loan (the ADB Loan) in an aggregate amount equivalent 

to one hundred million dollars ($100,000,000) to assist in financing 

Part A.1 of the Project on terms and conditions set forth in an 
agreement entered into between ADB and the Borrower (the ADB Loan 

Agreement) on April 8, 1994; 

(Cc) the Borrower intends to contract from the Japan Export 

Import Bank (JEXIM) a loan (the JEXIM Loan) in an aggregate amount 

equivalent to one hundred million dollars ($100,000,000) to assist 

in financing Part A.1 of the Project on terms and conditions set 

forth in an agreement to be entered into between JEXIM and the 

Borrower (the JEXIM Loan Agreement) ; 

(D) the Borrower has contracted from the European Investment 

Bank (EZB) a loan (the EIB Loan) in an aggregate amount equivalent 

to forty million ECU (ECU 40,000,000), to assist in financing Part 

A.3 of the Project on terms and conditions set forth in an agreement 

entered into between EIB and the Borrower (the EIB Loan Agreement) 

on February 14, 1994; 

(E) by an agreement (the Guarantee Agreement) of even date 

herewith between the Guarantecr and the Bank, the Guarantor has agreed 

to guarantee the obligations of the Borrower in respect of the Loan; 

and 

WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the 

foregoing, to extend the Loan to the Borrower upon the terms and 

conditions set forth in this Agreement;

SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objectives of the Project are to assist the Guarantor and 

the Borrower to achieve greater efficiency in the operation of the 

energy gas sector and increase the utilization of domestic natural 

gas resources through: 

(a) the expansion of the Borrower’s gas transmission system; and 

(b) the strengthening of the Borrower’s institutional, commer- 

cial and financial capabilities. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such 
modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from 

time to time to achieve such objectives: 

Part A: Pipeline Construction 

1. (a) Provision of pipeline materials; and 

(b) Carrying out pipeline coating works. 

2. Construction of a 36-inch diameter, 425 km long submarine pipe- 

line from an existing platform at the Erawan field to the onshore 

receiving facilities at Rayong. 

3. Construction of a 28-inch diameter, 110 km long underground 

pipeline from the Rayong facilities to the Block valve No. 6, located 

in the proximity of EGAT’s power plant at Bang Pakong. 

4. Relocation of Affected Persons from the land acquired by the 
Borrower for the construction of the onshore pipeline and related 

facilities. 

Part B: Facilities 

1. Modification and expansion of the existing supervisory control 

and data acquisition system and the pipeline telecommunications 

network. 

2. Construction of facilities, including. scraper launchers and 

receivers, slug catchers, pressure and flow controls, pipe manifolding, 

tie-ins and offshore compression st:ation, at: 

(a) Erawan riser platform, compression and central processing 

platforms;
{b) Rayong receiving terminal; and 

(c) Block Valve No. 6. 

Pa Cc: Engineering and Project Supervision 

Provision of services for the carrying out of basic design 

detailed erigineering preparation of bid documents, procurement of goods 

and works, supervision of the construction and commissioning of facili- 
ties pertaining to the facilities described under Part B above. 

Part D: Managerial Capabilities 

1. Provision of training for the staff of the Borrower in, inter 

alia, the following areas: project analysis including pricing, 
investment programming, corporate policy and planning, corporate 

finance and financial systems. - 

2. Carrying. out of an assessment of the Borrower’s planning and 

financial systems, and preparation of an action plan to improve said 

systems. 

Part Es ° ate Rest turin nd tu 

1. Carrying out of a study for the future corporate restructuring 

and institutional strengthening of the Borrower and preparation of an 

action plan therefor. 

2. Carrying out of other studies in the petroleum sector in accord- 

ance with terms of reference to be agreed between the Borrower and the 

Bank. 

Part F: Safety Standards and Env nmental_ R ° 

Provision of training for the Borrower’s environmental and safety 
staff to implement and enforce compliance with the recommendations of 
the Study on safety engineering and environmental standards. 

The Project is expected to be completed by June 30, 1997.

SCHEDULE 4 

Procurement and Consultants’ Services 

Section I. Procurement of Works 

Part As International Competitive Bidding 

1. Works shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance 

with procedures consistent with those set forth in Sections I and II 

of the "Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits” 

published by the Bank in May 1992 (the Guidelines). 

2. In the procurement of works in accordance with this Part A, the 

Borrower shall use the relevant standard bidding documents issued by 

the Bank, with such modifications thereto as the Bank shall have agreed 

to be necessary for the purpose of the Project. Where no relevant 

standard bidding documents have been issued by the Bank, the Borrower 

shall use bidding documents based on other internationally recognized 

standard forms agreed with the Bank. 

Part B: Review by the Bank of Procurement Decisions 

1. Review of invitations to bid and of proposed awards and final 
contracts: 

(a) With respect to each contract for the provision of works, 

estimated to cost the equivalent of $1,000,000 or more, the procedures 

set forth in paragraphs 2 and 4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall 

apply. 

(b) With respect to each contract not governed by the preced- 

ing péragraph, the procedures set forth in paragraphs 3 and 4 of 

Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply. 

2. The figure of 15% is hereby specified for purposes of paragraph 

4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines. 

Section II. Employment of Consultants 

1. In order to assist the Borrower in strengthening its managerial 

and institutional capabilities referred to under Parts C, D, E and F 

of the Project, the Borrower shall employ consultants whose qualifica- 

tions, experience and terms and conditions of employment shall be 
satisfactory to the Bank. Such consultants shall be selected in 

accordance with principles and procedures satisfactory to the Bank on 

the, basis of the “Guidelines for the Use of Consultants by World Bank 
Borrowers and by the World Bank as Executing Agency" published by the
Bank in August 1981 (the Consultant Guidelines). For complex, time- 

based assignments, the Borrower shall employ such consultants under 

contracts using the standard form of contzact for consuitants’ services 

issued by the Bank, with such modifications as shall have been agreed 

by the Bank. Where no relevant standard contract documents have been 

issued by the Bank, the Borrower shall use other standard forms agreed 

with the Bank. 

2. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Section, 

the provisions of the Consultant Guidelines requiring prior Bank review 

or approval of budgets, short lists, selection procedures, letters of 

invitation, proposals, evaluation reports and contracts, shall not 
apply to: 

(a) contracts for the employment of consulting firms estimated 

to cost less than $100,000 equivalent each; or 

(b) contracts for the employment of individuals estimated to 

cost less than $50,000 equivalent each. However, said exceptions to 

prior Bank review shall not apply to: 

(i) the terms cf reference for such contracts; 

(ii) single-source selection of consulting firms; 

(iii) assignments of a critical nature, as reasonably 

determined by the Bank; 

(iv) amendments to contracts for the employment of 

consulting firms raising the contract value to 

$100,000 equivalent or above; or 

(v) amendments to contracts for the employment of 

individual consuitants raising the contract value 

to $50,000 equivalent or above.
SCHEDULE 5 

Implementation Program 

Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, the Borrower shall: 

1. (a) not later than July 31, 1995 furnish to the Bank for its 

review and comments the findings and recommendations of the Corporate 

Restructuring and Institutional Strengthening Study referred to under 

Part E of the Project; 

(b) not later than September 30, 1995 exchange views with the 

Bank with respect to recommendations of said study; and 

(c) promptly thereafter, on the basis of said exchange of 

views, take all measures required on its part, to ensure implementation 

of the study’s recommendations, taking into consideration the Bank’s 

comments thereon. 

2. For the purposes of Part F of the Project take ali necessary 

measures required on its part and to ensure implementation of: 

(a) the Action Plan and the associated monitoring activities 

contained in the Environmental Assessment Report; and 

(b) the recommendations of the study on safety engineering and 

environmental standards for PTT’s gas transmission operations; 

3. Not later than March 31, 1995, employ consultants, in accordance 

with the provisions of Section II of Schedule 4 of this Agreement, to 

carry out the training programs referred to under Parts D and F of th 

Project. 

4. Commencing on November 1, 1994, and for each of its fiscal years 

thereafter, until the completion of the Project, prepare and submit 

to the Bank for its review and comments, quarterly progress reports 

on Project implementation.

the executed Bongkot Gas Sales Agreement, Unocal Gas Sale Agreement

reserves, under terms and conditions satisfactory to the Bank.

the provisions of Section II of Schedule 4 to this Agreement.
