ARTICLE II 

The Loan 

Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the 

terms and conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement, 

various currencies that shall have an aggregate value equivalent to 

the amount of fifty million Dollars ($50,000,000), being the sum of 

withdrawals of the proceeds of the Loan, with each withdrawal valued 

by the Bank as of the date of such withdrawal. 

Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from 

the Loan Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to 

this Agreement for expenditures made (or, if the Bank shall so 

agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of goods and 
services required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this 

Agreement and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan. 

(b) The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, 
open and maintain in Dollars a special deposit account in BCP on 
terms and conditions satisfactory to the Bank. Deposits into, and 
payments out of, the Special Account shall be made in accordance 

with the provisions of Schedule 5 to this Agreement. 

Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be December 31, 2002, or 

such later date as the Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly 
notify the Borrower of such later date. 

Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment 

charge at the rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per 
annum on the principal amount of the Loan not withdrawn from time to 
time. 

Section 2.05. (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the 

principal amount of the Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to 
time, at a rate for each Interest Period equal to the Cost of 
Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding 
Semester, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of the 
dates specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower 

shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount outstanding 
during the preceding Interest Period, calculated at the rate 
applicable during such Interest Period. 

(b) As soon as practicable after the end of each Semester, 

the Bank shall notify the Borrower of the Cost of Qualified 
Borrowings determined in respect of such Semester. 

(c) For the purposes of this Section: 

(i) "Interest Period” means a six-month period ending 

on the date immediately preceding each date 
specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement, 
beginning with the Interest Period in which this 
Agreement is signed. 

(ii) "Cost of Qualified Borrowings" means the cost, as 
reasonably determined by the Bank and expressed 

as a percentage per annum, of the outstanding 

borrowings of the Bank drawn down after June 30, 

1982, excluding such borrowings or portions 

thereof as the Bank has allocated to fund: (A) 

the Bank’s investments; and (B) loans which may 

be made by the Bank after July 1, 1989 bearing 
interest rates determined otherwise than as 

provided in paragraph (a) of this Section. 

(iii) "Semester" means the first six months or the 

second six months of a calendar year. 

(d) On such date as the Bank may specify by no less than six 

months’ notice to the Borrower, paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) (iii) of 

this Section shall be amended to read as follows: 

"(a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the 

principal amount of the Loan withdrawn and outstanding 
from time to time, at a rate for each Quarter equal to 

the Cost of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect 

of the preceding Quarter, plus one-half of one percent 

(1/2 of 1%). On each of the dates specified in Section 

2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower shall pay interest 

accrued on the principal amount outstanding during the 

preceding Interest Period, calculated at the rates 
applicable during such Interest Period." 

"(b) As soon as practicable after the end of each 

Quarter, the Bank shall notify the Borrower of the Cost 

of Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of such 

Quarter." 

"(c) (iii) ‘Quarter’ means a three-month period 

commencing on Januarv 1, April 1, July 1 or October 1 in 

a calendar year." 

Section 2.06. Interest and other charges shall be payable 

semiannually on June 15 and December 15 in each year. 

Section 2.07. The Borrower shall repay the principal amount of 

the Loan in accordance with the amortization schedule set forth in 

Schedule 3 to this Agreement.

SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objectives of the Project are to: (a) establish the 

institutional framework necessary to resolve the major agricultural 
and natural resources problems in the Project Area; (b) obtain 

relevant information and implement a system of natural resources 

protection; (c) assist the Small Farmers in the Project Area in: (i) 

creating sustainable and diversified agricultural production 
systems; (ii) adopting natural resources conservation measures 

through appropriate land use planning; and (iii) improving their 

productivity through processing and marketing of agricultural 
products; and (d) encourage the participation of the rural community 

living in the Project Area in the management of natural resources. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such 

modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon 
from time to time to achieve such objectives: 

Part A: Agricultural Development 

l. Carrying out of a program (including the acquisition of goods 

and the provision of technical assistance to MAG’s staff required 
therefor) aimed at the restructuring of the agricultural production 
and natural resources management in the Project Area towards 

sustainable, conservation-oriented agriculture, through the 
activities included in the following plans: 

(a) a plan to slow down and reverse the degradation of soil 

and other natural resources; 

(b) a plan to support crop production and to provide 

specific alternatives for agricultural production; and 

(c) a plan to develop and test agro-forestry systems. 

2. Carrying out of a program (including the acquisition of goods 

and the provision of technical assistance to MAG’s staff required 
therefor) to expand the Protected Area, conduct forestry research 
and enforce environmental legislation, through the activities 
included in the following plans:  

(a) a plan to protect, conserve and recuperate the genetic 

diversity of the Project Area through reforestation and technology 
generation; 

(b) a plan to: (i) enforce the Borrower’s environmental 

legislation; and (ii) strengthen the Borrower’s environmental 

institutions; and 

(c) a plan to strengthen the Borrower’s institutional 
presence in the Protected Area and to increase the size of the 

Protected Area. 

3. Carrying out of a program (including the acquisition of goods 

and the provision of technical assistance to MAG’s staff required 

therefor) to develop agro-industry and marketing of agricultural 

products for the benefit of agricultural producers, by means of the 
activities included in the following plans: 

(a) a plan to support the development of municipalities, 

cooperatives and other rural community organizations engaged in 

agriculture and small-scale industrial activities; and 

(b) a plan to provide technical assistance to cooperatives 

and other entities which carry out agro-industry and marketing 
activities. 

4. Financing of On-~farm Investments. 

Part B: Indigenous Communities 

1. Carrying out of a program (including the provision of external 

auditing services to INDI, acquisition of goods and the provision of 
technical assistance to INDI’s staff required therefor) of 

regularization of land tenure and expansion of areas reserved to the 
indigenous population. 

2. Carrying out of a program aimed at the diversification and 

improvement of agricultural production. 

3. Carrying out of a program of improvement of health services to 

the indigenous population. 

4. Carrying out of a program of promotion of primary and informal 

adult education to the indigenous population.
Part C: Infrastructure Development 
l. Realignment, reconstruction and improvement of about 455 
kilometers of rural roads. 

2. Carrying out of a maintenance program for the roads realigned, 
reconstructed or improved under Part C.1l of the Project, including 

the acquisition of goods required therefor. 

Part D: Institutional Development 

l. Provision of technical assistance and training to MAG’s staff 

(DINCAP, UCTAP, CICAR and CRAE) to strengthen MAG’s institutional 

capacity, including the acquisition of goods and the provision of 
external auditing services required therefor. 

2. Carrying out of special assessment studies to capitalize 

experience coming from the implementation of Parts A and E of the 

Project, to be coordinated by DINCAP. 

Part E: Agricultural Credit 

l. Financing of Subprojects. 

2. Provision of technical assistance and training to CAH’s staff 

to strengthen CAH’s institutional capacity, including the 

acquisition of goods required therefor.

paragraphs 1 through 4 of Appendix 2 thereto.

Section I. CAH Subsidiary Grant Agreement 

The CAH Subsidiary Grant Agreement shall provide, inter alia, 

for the obligation of CAH to: 

(a) finance up to 70% of the cost of each Subproject 
provided, however, that subloans to Small Farmers shall not exceed 

the following limits: (i) to an individual, up to $1,700 equivalent; 

(ii) to an AUCA, up to $27,000 equivalent; and (iii) to a 

Cooperative, up to $82,000 equivalent, provided, however, that the 

proceeds of the Loan to be allocated to each Subloan, shall not be 

used by CAH to refinance or restructure CAH’s loans in arrears. 

(b) charge an interest rate on each Subloan which shall at 

all times exceed the higher of the average rate for the previous 
twelve months for time deposits (Depdésitos a plazo) in the 

Borrower’s banking system or the previous twelve months domestic 

inflation rate, measured by the Borrower’s consumer price index 

published monthly by the BCP in its bulletin; and 

(c) prior to the review of any application for a Subloan 
require the Small Farmer to furnish a farm development plan to CAH, 

which shall include, inter alia, actions, satisfactory to CAH, 

concerning conservation and natural resource management.
