SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objectives of the Project are to: (a) improve the quality, efficiency and 
coverage of Secondary Education; and (b) expand the institutional capacity of MEC to 
monitor the enrollment of students, evaluate the students’ cognitive achievement and 
design educational policies. - 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof 
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to iime to achieve such 
objectives: 

Part A: Institutional Strengthening 

1. Design and implementation of an educational statistics system to monitor the 
performance of the education in Paraguay (with the exception of higher education), 
including: (a) the acquisition and utilization of the equipment required therefor; (b) the 
provision of training to MEC’s personnel in the management and implementation of such 

system; and (c) the design and distribution cf manuals to disseminate the information 
produced by such system. 

2. Design and implementation of a national student assessment system to periodically 
obtain information on student’s cognitive achievement, including the provision of training 
of MEC’s personnel in the implementation of such system. 

3. Design and implementation of a cognitive achievement test (based on the 
implementation of the system referred to in Part A.2 above) for students attending the 
ninth and twelfth grades of schools in Paraguay, including the provision of training to 
MEC’s personnel in the implementation of such test and the acquisition and utilization of 
the equipment required therefor. 

4, Carrying out of a nationwide campaign to inform school principals, school 

supervisors, teachers and the general public with respect to the relevance of the system 
referred to in Part A.2 above. 

5. Carrying out of the following studies: (a) a tracer study of school leavers from 
Secondary Education and an analysis of Paraguay’s labor market with respect to the future 

needs of Secondary Education; (b) a study on the financing framework of the education 
sector in Paraguay; (c) a study on the status, organization and capacity of school teachers 

of Secondary Education; and (d) a study on the impact of the Project. 

6. Carrying out of Research Proposals. 

7. Provision of technical assistance to MEC in order to assist MEC in the 
development of options to decentralize the education system in Paraguay. 

8. Organization of seminars to present and discuss the studies, the Research 
Proposals and the technical assistance referred to in Parts A.5, A.6 and A.7 above, 
respectively, including the production and distribution of reports and materials required 

therefor. a 

Part B: Textbooks, Educational Materials and Curricular Support 

1. Acquisition and distribution of about 900,000 textbooks in the areas of 

mathematics, Spanish and natural sciences for students of public schools attending seventh 

through ninth grades. 

2. Design of a mechanism for the ongoing acquisition of textbooks after the 
completion of the Project. 

3. Acquisition and distribution of: (a) texts,reference books and specialized books 
for about 600 new public schools libraries of Secondary Education; and (b) supplemental 
books (to complement existing books) for about 200 existing public schools libraries of 
Secondary Education. 

4, Provision of trajhing to school librarians, teachers and students in the 

management, maintenance and updating of school libraries. 

5. Acquisition and distribution of basic learning materials for students attending 

seventh through ninth grades in about 800 public schools. 

6. Acquisition and distribution of teacher training reference materials for each DPU 
to enable each DPU to carry out the in-service teacher training referred to in Part C.1 
below. 

7. Provision of technical assistance to the personnel of the Department of Curriculum 
within MEC to assist such personnel in: (a) the design of general curricula; (b) the design 

of criteria and quality standards to select textbooks and learning materials; (c) the design 
of methodologies for evaluating the use and impact of learning materials; (d) the design 
and use of mini-laboratories to conduct natural science experiments; and (e) core skill 

areas, including the provision of training required therefor. 

Part C: Training 

1. Provision of in-service training to teachers of public schools of Secondary 
Education in order to enable such teachers to: (a) implement curriculum reforms in 
seventh through ninth grades; and (b) improve the teaching techniques to use the 
textbooks and learning materials referred to in Parts B.1 and B.5 above. 

2. Provision of training to school supervisors, pedagogic coordinators, evaluators 
(including other specialized personnel of MEC), school principals and their non-teaching 
staff of public schools of Secondary Education, on: (a) the objectives and methodology 
of the education reform; (b) the curriculum content and its application in study plans and 
classroom sessions; (c) the administration of schools; (d) the use, conservation and cost 

recovery mechanisms for the sustainability of learning materials; and (e) the norms, 
procedures and methods for increasing the participation of teachers, students and parents 
in school management. | 

3. Provision of technical assistance and training to Parents’ Associations to 
strengthen their management capacity in school activities. 

4. Production and distribution of training and information materials and the 
acquisition and utilization of the equipment required to provide the training referred to in 

Parts C.1 through C.3 above. 

5. Provision of technical assistance to MEC to strengthen the capacity of MEC to 
coordinate the training activities referred to in Parts C.1 through C.4 above. 

Part D: Infrastructure 

1. Provision of technical assistance to MEC to strengthen the capacity ef MEC to 

carry out Part D.2 of the Project. 

2. Carrying out of Eligible Subprojects. 

Part E: Pilot Innovative Subprojects 

1. Provision of technical assistance to MEC to assist MEC in the design of the © 
eligibility criteria and operational procedures referred to in paragraph 3(d) of Schedule 
1 to this Agreement. 

2. Carrying out of Pilot Innovative Subprojects.

(c) "Interest Payment Date” means any date specified in Section 2.06 of this 

Agreement. 

(d) "Rate Fixing Date" means, for each Disbursed Amount, the first day of 
the Interest Period next following the Interest Period in which such Disbursed Amount 

is withdrawn. 

B. Interest 

1. The principal amount of the Loan shall be divided into Disbursed Amounts. 

Interest shall accrue on each Disbursed Amount outstanding from time to time at a rate 
based on a floating rate index prior to its Rate Fixing Date and at a fixed rate from its 
Rate Fixing Date, as described in paragraph 2 of this Part B. 

2. (a) From the date of withdrawal of each amount of each Disbursed Amount 
withdrawn and outstanding, to but not including the Rate Fixing Date for such Disbursed 
Amount, interest on each such amount shall accrue, at a rate equal to the applicable: 

(1) LIBOR Base Rate; plus 

(ii) LIBOR Total Spread. 

(b) From the Rate Fixing Date for each Disbursed Amount until final 
repayment thereof, interest on such Disbursed Amount shall accrue, at a rate equal to the 
applicable: 

(i) Fixed Base Rate; plus 

(ii) Fixed Total Spread. 

3. For purposes of paragraph 2 of this Part B, the following terms have the 

following meanings. 

(a) "LIBOR Base Rate" means, for the Interest Period in which a Disbursed 
Amount is withdrawn, the London interbank offered rate for six-month deposits in Dollars 

for value the first day of such Interest Period (or, in the case of the initial Interest Period,

SCHEDULE 4 

Procurement and Consultants’ Services 

Section I. Procurement of Goods and Works 

Part A: General 

Works and goods shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section 
I of the "Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits" published by 
the Bank in January 1995 (the Guidelines) and the following provisions of this Section, 

as applicable. 

Part B: International Competitive Bidding 

1. Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, works and goods shall be 
procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of Section II of the 

Guidelines and paragraph 5 of Appendix 1 thereto. 

2. The following provisions shail apply to the works and goods to be procured under 
contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Part B, as 
appropriate. 

(a) Grouping of contracts 

To the extent practicable, contracts for works and goods shall be grouped into bid 
packages estimated to cost each more than the equivalent of $2,000,000 and $250,000, 

respectively. | 

(b) Two-stage bidding procedure 

The bidding procedure for textbooks shall be carried out in two stages in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2.6 of the Guidelines. 

(c) Preference for domestically manufactured goods 

The provisions of paragraphs 2.54 and 2.55 of the Guidelines and Appendix 2 
thereto shall apply to goods manufactured in the territory of the Borrower. 

Part C: Other Procurement Procedures 

1. Limited International Bidding 

Hardware, software applications, photocopiers and vehicles shall be grouped into 
bid packages estimated to cost more than $50,000 which the Bank agrees can only be 
purchased from a limited number of suppliers, may be procured under contracts awarded 
in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3.2 of the Guidelines. 

2. National Competitive Bidding 

(a) Works estimated to cost the equivalent of $2,000,000 or less but more 
than the equivalent of $75,000 per contract, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to 
$10,300,000, may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions 

of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines. 

(b) Goods (other than those set forth in Part C.1 hereof or textbooks) 
estimated to cost the equivalent of $250,000 or less but more than the equivalent of 

$50,000 per contract, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to $700,000, may be procured 
under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of 
the Guidelines. 

(c) All procurement of works and goods pursuant to this Part C.2 shall be 

carried out using standard bidding documents acceptable to the Bank. 

(d) In addition and without limitation to any other provision set forth in this 
Schedule or the Guidelines, the following principles and rules of procurement shall 
expressly govern all procurement of goods and works referred to in this Part C.2: 

(i) Foreign bidders shall not, as a condition for submitting bids, be 
required to enter into a joint venture agreement with local 
bidders. 

(ii) Foreign bidders may be required as a prerequisite for bidding, 
to be registered with local authorities, provided, however, that 

the information to be requested by the Borrower to such bidders 
is of such nature that it will not discourage their participation. 

(iii) Contract prices for works and goods may be adjusted even if . 
such prices have increased by less than 15% of the bid price. — 

(iv) There will be no prescribed minimum number of bids to be 
submitted in order for a contract to be subsequently awarded. 

(v) Foreign bidders shall not be required to notarize their bidding 
documents with Paraguayan authorities as a prerequisite of 
bidding. 

(vi) Foreign bidders shall not be required to hire local personnel, 
except for unskilled personnel. 

3. National or International Shopping 

Goods (other than textbooks) estimated to cost the equivalent of $50,000 or less 

per contract, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to $1,300,000, may be procured under 

contracts awarded on the basis of national or international shopping procedures in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines. 

4. Procurement of Small Works 

Works, estimated to cost the equivalent of $75,000 or less but more than the 

equivalent of $25,000 per contract, and not to exceec’ $500,000 in the aggregate may be 

procured under lump sum, fixed price contracts awarded on the basis of quotations. 
obtained from three qualified domestic contractors in response to a written invitation. The” 
invitation shall include a detailed description of the works, including basic specifications, 
the required completion date, a basic form of agreement acceptable to the Bank, and 
relevant drawings, where applicable. The award shall be inade to the contractor who 
offers the lowest price quotation for the required work, and who has the experience and 
resources to successfully complete the contract. 

5. Parents’ Association Participation and Pilot Innovative Subprojects 

(a) Works (procured by Parents’ Associations on behalf of MEC if requested 
by MEC to do so under Eligible Subprojects) estimated to cost the equivalent of $25,000 
or less per contract, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to $8,300,000, may be procured 
by direct contracting in accordance with procedures acceptable to the Bank. 

(b) Goods for Pilot Innovative Subprojects estimated to cost the equivalent 
of $5,000 or less per contract, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to $900,000, for 
which at least three price quotations can not be obtained under the provisions of Part C.3 
hereof, may be procured by direct contracting in accordance with procedures acceptable 
to the Bank. 

Part D: Review by the Bank of Procurement Decisions 

1. Procurement Planning 

Prior to the issuance of any invitations to bid for contracts, the proposed 
procurement plan for the Project shall be furnished to the Bank for its review and 
approval, in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of Appendix 1 to the 
Guidelines. Procurement of all works, goods and textbooks (including the distribution of 
textbooks) shall be undertaken in accordance with such procurement plan as shall have 
been approved by the Bank, and with the provisions of said paragraph 1. 

2. Prior Review 

With respect to each contract to be awarded under Parts B, C.1 and C.2 of this 
Section, the procedures set forth in paragraphs 2 and 3 of Appendix | to the Guidelines 
shall apply. 

3. Post Review 

With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of this Part, the 
procedures set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.

1. Consultants’ services shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance

in the table in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; 

(b) the term "eligible expenditures" means expenditures in respect of the 
reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Project and to be financed out of

to Section 5.02 of the General Conditions] shall be equal to or exceed the equivalent of

expenditures in accordance with the provisions of this Schedule.

has been duly opened, withdrawals of the Authorized Allocation and subsequent

aggregate amount of the Authorized Allocation. On the basis of such request or requests,

(b) (i) For replenishment of the Special Account, the Borrower skall 
furnish to the Bank requests for deposits into the Special 
Account at such intervals as the Bank shall specify. 

(ii) Prior to or at the time of each such request, the Borrower shall 

furnish to the Bank the documents and other evidence required 
pursuant to paragraph 4 of this Schedule for the payment or 
payments in respect of which replenishment is requested. On 
the basis of each such request, the Bank shall, on behalf of the 

Borrower, withdraw from the Loan Account and deposit into 
the Special Account such amount as the Borrower shall have 
requested and as shall have been shown by said documents and 

other evidence to have been paid out of the Special Account for 

eligible expenditures.

the respective eligible Categories, and in the respective equivalent amounts, as shall have 

been justified by said documents and other evidence.

Borrower shall, at such time as the Bank shall reasonably request, furnish to the Bank 
such documents and other evidence showing that such payment was made exclusively for 
eligible expenditures. |
