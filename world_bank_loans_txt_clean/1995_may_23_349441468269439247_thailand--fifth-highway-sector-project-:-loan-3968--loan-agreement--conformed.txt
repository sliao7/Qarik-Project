ARTICLE II

The Loan

Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the
terms and conditions set forth or referred to in this Loan
Agreement, various currencies that shall have an aggregate value
equivalent to the amount of one hundred fifty million dollars
($150,000,000), being the sum of withdrawals of the proceeds of the
Loan, with each withdrawal valued by the Bank as of the date of such
withdrawal.

Section 2.02. The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from the
Loan Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this
Agreement for expenditures made or, if the Bank shall so agree, 
to

be made in respect of the reasonable cost of goods and services

required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this Agreement

and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan.

Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be June 30, 2000 or such

later date as the Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly

notify the Borrower of such later date.

Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment

charge at the rate of three-fourths of one percent (3/4 of 1%) per

annum on the principal amount of the Loan not withdrawn from time to

time.

Section 2.05. (a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the

principal amount of the Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to

time, at a rate for each Interest Period equal to the Cost of

Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding

Semester, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of the

dates specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the Borrower

shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount outstanding

during the preceding Interest Period, calculated at the rate

applicable during such Interest Period.

(b) As soon as practicable after the end of each Semester,

the Bank shall notify the Borrower of the Cost of Qualified

Borrowings determined in respect of such Semester.

(c) For the purposes of this Section:

(i) "Interest Period" means a six-month period ending
on the date immediately preceding each date

specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement,

beginning with the Interest Period in which this

Agreement is signed.

(ii) "Cost of Qualified Borrowings" means the cost, as

reasonably determined by the Bank and expressed

as a percentage per annum, of the outstanding

borrowings of the Bank drawn down after June 30,

1982, excluding such borrowings or portions

thereof as the Bank has allocated to fund:

(A) the Bank's investments; and
(B) loans which may be made by the Bank after
July 1, 1989 bearing interest rates
determined otherwise than as provided in
paragraph (a) of this Section.

(iii) "Semester" means the first six months or the
second six months of a calendar year.

(d) On such date as the Bank may specify by no less than six
months' notice to the Borrower, paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) (iii) of
this Section shall be amended to read as follows:

"(a) The Borrower shall pay interest on the principal
amount of the Loan withdrawn and outstanding from time to
time, at a rate for each Quarter equal to the Cost of
Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of the preceding
Quarter, plus one-half of one percent (1/2 of 1%). On each of
the dates specified in Section 2.06 of this Agreement, the
Borrower shall pay interest accrued on the principal amount
outstanding during the preceding Interest Period, calculated
at the rates applicable during such Interest Period."

"(b) As soon as practicable after the end of each
Quarter, the Bank shall notify the Borrower of the Cost of
Qualified Borrowings determined in respect of such Quarter."

"(c) (iii) 'Quarter' means a three-month period
commencing on January 1, April 1, July 1 or October 1 in a
calendar year."

Section 2.06. Interest and other charges shall be payable
semi-annually on January 15 and July 15 in each year.

Section 2.07. The Borrower shall repay the principal amount of
the Loan in accordance with the amortization schedule set forth in
Schedule 3 to this Agreement.

ARTICLE III

Execution of the Project

Section 3.01. (a) The Borrower declares its commitment to the
objectives of the Project as set forth in Schedule 2 to this
Agreement, and, to this end, shall carry out the Project with due
diligence and efficiency and in conformity with appropriate

engineering, administrative, financial and environmental practices,

and shall provide, promptly as needed, the funds, facilities,
services and other resources required for the Project.

(b) Without limitation upon the provisions of paragraph (a)

of this Section and except as the Borrower and the Bank shall

otherwise agree, the Borrower shall carry out the Project in

accordance with the Implementation Program set forth in Schedule 5

to this Agreement.

Section 3.02. Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree,

procurement of the goods, works and consultants' services required

for the Project and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan

shall be governed by the provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement.

Section 3.03. For the purposes of Section 9.08 of the General

Conditions and without limitation thereto, the Borrower shall:

(a) prepare, on the basis of the guidelines acceptable to

the Bank, and furnish to the Bank not later than six (6) months

after the Closing Date or such later date as may be agreed for this

purpose between the Borrower and the Bank, a plan for the future

operation of the Project; and

(b) afford the Bank a reasonable opportunity to exchange

views with the Borrower on said plan.

Section 3.04. The Borrower shall, by March 31, 1996 and,

thereafter, by March 31 of each subsequent year until completion of

the Project:

(a) carry out, jointly with the Bank, a progress review of

the scope and implementation of the Seventh Highway Plan and the

Action Plans; and

(b) consult with the Bank with regard to the scope and

nature of activities to be carried out under the Action Plans in the

course of the following fiscal year, paying due regard to the

Bank's comments and recommendations.
ARTICLE IV

Financial Covenants

Section 4.01. (a) The Borrower shall maintain or cause to be
maintained records and accounts adequate to reflect in accordance
with sound accounting practices the operations, resources and
expenditures in respect of the Project of the departments or
agencies of the Borrower responsible for carrying out the Project or
any part thereof.

(b) The Borrower shall:

(1) have the records and accounts referred to in
paragraph (a) of this Section for each fiscal
year audited, in accordance with appropriate
auditing principles consistently applied, by
independent auditors acceptable to the Bank;

(ii) furnish to the Bank as soon as available, but in
any case not later than nine months after the end
of each such year, the report of such audit by
said auditors, of such scope and in such detail
as the Bank shall have reasonably requested; and

(iii) furnish to the Bank such other information
concerning said records and accounts and the
audit thereof as the Bank shall from time to time
reasonably request.

(c) For all expenditures with respect to which withdrawals
from the Loan Account were made on the basis of statements of
expenditure, the Borrower shall:

(i) maintain or cause to be maintained, in accordance
with paragraph (a) of this Section, records and
accounts reflecting such expenditures;

(ii) retain, until at least one year after the Bank
has received the audit report for the fiscal year
in which the last withdrawal from the Loan
Account was made, all records (contracts, orders,
invoices, bills, receipts and other documents)
evidencing such expenditures;
(iii) enable the Bank's representatives to examine such
records; and

(iv) ensure that such records and accounts are
included in the annual audit referred to in

paragraph (b) of this Section and that the report
of such audit contains a separate opinion by said
auditors as to whether the statements of
expenditure submitted during such fiscal year,
togRther with the procedures and internal
controls involved in their preparation, can be
relied upon to support the related withdrawals.

SCEULE 2

Description of the Project

The objectives of the Project are to assist the Borrower to:
(a) improve safety, and mitigate the environmental impact, of its
road sub-sector; and (b) improve the quality of service of its road
network.

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such
modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon
from time to time to achieve such objectives:

Part A: Increasing the Capacity of the Road Network

Carrying out of civil work Sub-projects for capacity
improvement, rehabilitation, and/or safety improvement of selected
sections of the road network, pursuant to the Seventh Highway Plan.

Part B: Improvinc the Institutional Capacity of DOH

Development and implementation of an action plan for the
financing of the expansion of the Borrower's highway network.

Part C: Improvinc the Borrower's Environmental Manacement Capacity

1. Improving the institutional capacity of DOH's Environmental
Unit and OEPP to design and implement an environmental assessment
policy framework and guidelines to, inter alia:

(a) carry out and review environmental impact assessments
for highway works;

(b) design and implement appropriate environmental measures
to mitigate the negative impact of road transportation; and

(c) monitor and evaluate the effectiveness of said
assessments and measures.

2. Design of a policy framework and guidelines to assist DOH in
resolving land acquisition, resettlement and compensation issues
arising out of the planning, construction and operation of the
Borrower's highway network.
Part D: Implementation of the Action Plans

1. Improving the institutional capac.ty of the Implementing

Agencies to carry out the Action Plans, through the provision of

advisory services, equipment and training for the staff of said

Agencies.

2. Monitoring of implementation, and periodic review of the scope

of the Action Plans.

The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 1999.

SCHEDULE 4

Procurement and Consultants' Services

Section I. Procurement of Works

Part A: General

Works shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of
Section I of the "Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and
IDA Credits" published by the Bank in January 1995 (the Guidelines)
and the following provisions of this Section, as applicable.

Part B: International Comvetitive Bidding

1. Works shall be procured under contracts awarded in accordance
with the provisions of Section II of the Guidelines and paragraph 5
of Appendix 1 thereto.

2. The following provisions shall apply to works to be procured
under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of
paragraph 1 of this Part B.

(a) Prequalification

Bidders for the works included in Part A of the Project shall
be prequalified in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 2.9
and 2.10 of the Guidelines.

(b) Dispute Review Board

Each contract for works estimated to cost $50,000,000 equiva-
lent or more shall include the provisions for a dispute review board
set forth in the standard bidding documents for works referred to in
paragraph 2.12 of the Guidelines.

Part C: Review by the Bank of Procurement Decisions

1. Procurement P1anning

Prior to the issuance of any invitations to prequalify for
bidding or to bid for contracts, the proposed procurement plan for
the Project shall be furnished to the Bank for its review and
approval, in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of
Appendix 1 to the Guidelines. Procurement of all works shall be
undertaken in accordance with such procurement plan as shall have

been approved by the Bank, and with the provisions of said para-
graph 1.

2. Prior Review

With respect to each contract for works estimated to cost the

equivalent of $15,000,000 or more, the procedures set forth in

paragraphs 2 and 3 of Appendix 1 to the Guidelines shall apply.

3. Post Review

With respect to each contract not governed by paragraph 2 of

this Part, the procedure set forth in paragraph 4 of Appendix 1 to

the Guidelines shall apply.

Section II. Employment of Consultants

1. Consultants' services shall be procured under contracts

awarded in accordance with the provisions of the "Guidelines for the

Use of Consultants by the World Bank Borrowers and by the World Bank

as Executing Agency" published by the Bank in August 1981 (the

Consultant Guidelines). For complex, time-based assignments, such

contracts shall be based on the standard form of contract for

consultants' services issued by the Bank, with such modifications

thereto as shall have been agreed by the Bank. Where no relevant

standard contract documents have been issued by the Bank, other

standard forms acceptable to the Bank shall be used.

2.. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Section,

the provisions of the Consultant Guidelines requiring prior Bank

review or approval of budgets, short lists, selection procedures,

letters of invitation, proposals, evaluation reports and contracts,

shall not apply to:

(a) contracts for the employment of consulting firms

estimated to cost less than $100,000 equivalent each; or

(b) contracts for the employment of individual consultants

estimated to cost less than $50,000 equivalent each. However, said

exceptions to prior Bank review shall not apply to: (i) the terms of

reference for such contracts; (ii) single-source selection of

consulting firms; (iii) assignments of a critical nature, as

reasonably determined by the Bank; (iv) amendments to contracts for

the employment of consulting firms raising the contract value to
$100, 000 equivalent or above; or (v) amendments to contracts for the
employment of individual consultants raising the contract value to
$50,000 equivalent or above.
SCHEDULE 5

Implementation Program

Part A: Implementation Actions

1. In carrying out Part A of the Project, the Borrower shall

(a) throughout Project implementation, select Sub-projects to

be financed under the Project using technical, environmental and

economic criteria agreed between the Borrower and the Bank. Such

criteria to include, without limitation:

(i) a minimum economic rate of return of twelve (12%)

percent;

(ii) for each Sub-project involving the involuntary

resettlement of any persons due to the

acquisition of land by DOH to carry out such Sub-

project, a separate resettlement plan,

satisfactory to the Bank, prepared in

consultation with affected persons on the basis

of policies, planning principles and

institutional arrangements designed to improve

the living standards of said persons, together

with satisfactory operational procec- _3 adequate
to enable the Borrower to properly monitor and

evaluate the implementation of the resettlement

plan; and

(iii) for each Sub-project involving the acquisition of

new rights-of-way, an environmental impact

analysis of each proposed Sub-project, together

with a survey identifying any existing or

potential adverse environmental impact or effect,

and the proposed measures to be carried out to

remedy such impact and effect.

(b) by not later than December 31, of each year until

completion of the Project, furnish to the Bank, for its review and

approval, a list of proposed Sub-projects to be financed under the

Project, together with all supporting documentation, all in

accordance with the criteria set forth in Sub-paragraph 1 (a) above;

and
(c) promptly after receiving the Bank's approval thereof,
carry out the Sub-projects.

2. In carrying out Part B of the Project, the Borrower shall:

(a) by June 30, 1996, discuss with the Bank the results and
findings of the long-term strategic investment study dated May 24,
1995;

(b) prepare in consultation with the Bank and, not later than
June 30, 1997, furnish to the Bank, a time-bound action plan based
on the findings and results of the above-mentioned study; and

(c) promptly thereafter, take all necessary measures to
implement such action plan.

3. In carrying out Part C of the Project, the Borrower shall
approve and thereafter, implement in accordance with an
implementation timetable agreed between the Borrower and the Bank:

(a) the Environmental Assessment Guidelines; and

(b) the Land Acquisition, Resettlement and Compensation
Guidelines.

4. In carrying out Part D of the Project, the Borrower shall
implement the measures resulting from the periodic updating of the
Action Plans, with due regard to the monitorable performance
indicators and implementation timetable included in the said Action
Plans and acceptable to the Bank.

5. The Borrower shall:

(a) not later than March 31, 1998, carry out jointly with the
Bank, a mid-term Project implementation review to, inter alia:

(i) evaluate progress achieved by the Borrower, in
general, and by the Implementing Agencies, in
particular, in carrying out the Project, on the
basis of performance indicators agreed between
the Borrower and the Bank;

(ii) review and update the Action Plans;
(iii) evaluate progress achieved by the Borrower in

designing the Environmental Assessment Guidelines

and the Land Acquisition, Resettlement and

Compensation Guidelines referred to under Parts

C.1 and C.2, resnectively, of the Project, and

update the timetable for the implementation of

said Guidelines;

(iv) discuss and exchange views on all other related

activities in the road sub-sector financed by

other donor agencies; and

(v) exchange information among Implementing Agencies

and propose solutions to any current implementa-

tion issues.

(b) not later than January 31, 1998, furnish to the Bank,

through MOTC and APCU, a report in such detail as the Bank shall

reasonably request, including:

(i) an evaluation of progress achieved by the

Borrower in Project implementation; and

(ii) a draft work plan to be carried out until the

completion of the Project; and

(c) promptly after completing the mid-term review, carry out

those recommendations arising out of said review, as agreed between

the Borrower and the Bank.

Part B. Implementing Agencies

Responsibility for implementation of the Project shall be

vested in the Implementing Agencies as follows:

1. Ministry of Transport and Communications (MOTC)

(a) MOTC shall be responsible for the overall coordination

and implementation of the Project, in close cooperation with MOSTE.

(b) In discharging its responsibilities under the Project,

MOTC shall ensure that its Department of Highways (DOH) , Department

of Land Transport (LTD) and APCU, appoint and maintain, throughout

Project implementation, technical and counterpart staff 
in
sufficient numbers and with qualifications, experience and terms of
reference satisfactory to the Bank.

2. Department of Highways (DOH)

DOH shall be directly responsible for carrying out Parts A, B
and C of the Project.

3. Department of Land Transport (LTD)

(a) In carrying out its respective components of the Action
Plans, LTD shall take all necessary measures to ensure the continued
development of an effective program of vehicle inspections,
including LTD direct inspection and/or LTD supervision of licensed
vehicle inspection stations, and maintenance of the vehicle

registration system;

(b) LTD shall ensure that its staff participates in the
training programs provided under Part D.1 of the Project (Schedule
2 to this Agreement); and

(c) LTD shall, jointly with the Department of Pollution
Control (DPC) and in close collaboration with the Borrower's traffic
enforcement agencies, implement an on-road emissions inspection and
enforcement program to be carried out under the Project and as set
out in the Action Plan to Reduce Vehicle Emissions and Noise
Pollution referred to in Section 1.02 (a) (i) of this Agreement.

4. Department of Pollution Control (DPC)

DPC shall, through its Air Pollution Control Division:

(a) oversee and monitor air quality programs and air quality
monitoring systems carried out under the Project;

(b) update the Action Plan to Reduce Vehicle Emissions and
Noise Pollution in coordination with Action Plan implementing
agencies;

(c) prepare and submit for approval by the National
Environmental Board vehicle standards and emissions regulations; and

(d) carry out specific components of the Action Plans, as
agreed between the Borrower and the Bank.
5. Office of Environmental Policy and Planning (OEPP)

OEPP shall, through its Reviewing Committee:

(a) assist DOH in carrying out Part C.1 of the Project

(Schedule 2 to this Agreement);

(b) review and approve environmental impact asCessments (EAs)

carried out under the Project; and

(c) oversee compliance with such EAs.

Part C: Coordination among Implementing Agencies

The Borrower shall maintain, throughout Project

implementation, the following inter-institutional coordination

mechanism.

Action Plans' Coordinating Unit (APCU)

APCU shall assist the Implementing Agencies in:

(a) reviewing the implementation of procurement procedures

for goods and services financed under the Project, pursuant 
to the

provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement;

(b) reviewing and coordinating budget proposals prepared by

the Implementing Agencies to finance their annual work plans;

(c) monitoring and coordinating activities referred to under

Part D of the Project;

(d) preparing progress reports to be discussed with the Bank

pursuant to Section 3.04 of this Agreement; and

(e) updating the Action Plans.
