WHEREAS (A) in view of the provisions of the Borrower’s Constitution 

governing public metropolitan urban transpozt and the Borrower’s Law 8693, dated 
August 3, 1993, and published in the Borrower’s Official Gazette, dated Augrst 4, 1993, 

and in order to improve the operational efficiency and financial performance of suburban 
railways services and facilitate their integration with other transport services under state 
and municipal jurisdictions, the Borrower has decided to transfer the ownership, 
management and operation of Companhia Brasileira de Trens Urbanos (CBTU)’s assets 
in the metropolitan region of Recife (the STU-REC System) to the State of Pernambuco 

(the State); 

(B) the State has accepted the transfer of the STU-REC System, pursuant to the 
Law and on the terms and conditions set forth or referred to in the agreement entered into 
among the Borrower, CBTU and the State on June 1, 1995 (the Convénio Basico); 

(C) to attain the objectives of such transfer, the Borrower, the Bank, CBTU and 

the State have agreed that it is necessary that: (i) CBTU carry out the physical 
rehabilitation and financial restructuring of the STU-REC System together with the 

training program, studies and action plans included in the Project described in Schedule 
2 to this Agreement; and (ii) the State establish an entity (the PE Entity) as provided in 
the Shareholder’s Agreement (as hereinafter defined); 

(D) the Borrower, having satisfied itself as to the feasibility and priority of the 
Project, has requested the Bank to assist in the financing of the Project; 

(E) the Project will be carried out by CBTU with the Borrower’s and the State’s 

assistance and, as part of such assistance, the Borrower will make available to CBTU the 

proceeds of the Loan as provided in this Agreement; and 

WHEREAS the Bank has agreed, on the basis, inter alia, of the foregoing, to 

extend the Loan to the Borrower upon the terms and conditions set forth in this 
Agreement, in the Project Agreement of even date herewith between the Bank and CBTU 
and in the Shareholder’s Agreement of even date herewith between the Bank and the 
State; 

NOW THEREFORE the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objectives of the Project are: (a) to develop an integrated urban transport 
system for the RMR under EMTU’s coordination, including the adoption of common 
policies on urban transport pricing, regulation, financing, project evaluation and selection; 
(b) to complete the decentralization of the STU-REC System from the Borrower’s to the 

State’s level; (c) to reduce the adverse envirunmental impacts on the RMR caused by 

motor vehicles and to promote the use of non-motorized transport modes; and (d) to 
improve the accessibility of the poor to employment centers and health and education 
facilities. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof 
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives: 

Part A 

1. Improvement of STU-REC’s infrastructure and equipment, through: 

(a) construction of: (i) three integrated bus-to-rail terminals in the Central Line; 
and (ii) seven integrated bus-to-rail terminals and improvement of road accesses to 

stations in the South Line; 

(b) construction of a station at Timbi on the Central Line; 

(c) construction of ten stations and modernization of two existing stations on the 
Recife-Cajueiro Seco section of the South Line; 

(d) minor rehabilitation of five stations on the Cajueiro Seco-Cabo section of the 
South Line; 

(e) construction of: (i) ten bridges, two road viaducts and eight pedestrian 
over/underpasses on the Recife-Cajueiro Seco section of the South Line; (ii) two bridges 
and one road viaduct on the Cajueiro Seco-Cabo section of the South Line; and (111) four 
bridges, one road viaduct and two overpasses on the TIP-TIMBI section; 

(f) construction of about 4.5 kilometers of a double electrified track extension 

from the TIP to the Timbi station;
(g) construction of about 13.5 kilometers of double electrified track, from Recife 
to Cajueiro Seco; 

(h) relocation of about 11 kilometers of one of the existing tracks from 
Afogados to Cajueiro Seco; 

(i) construction of about 11 kilometers of a second track from Pontezinha to 

Cabo; 

(j) improvement of the electric system of the Central Line, including installation 
of circuit breakers and about 4.5 kilometers of electricity transmission lines and overhead 
catenary, and relocation of feeder power lines at the Cavaleiro yard; 

(k) improvement of the electric system of the South Line, including installation 

of about 14.5 kilometers of overhead catenary and about 13.5 kilometers of electricity 
transmission lines, and construction of two power substations and two sectionalizing 

cabins; 

(1) installation of a signalling system on the TIP-Timbi track extension referred 

to in (f) above, with the same characteristics of the system operating in the Central Line; 

(m) installation of (i) a telephone exchange system for the entire network; (ii) a 
telecommunications system on the TIP-Timbi extension referred to in (f) above; and (iii) 
about 13.5 kilometers of fiber optic cables and VHF radio network, a public address 
system, ticketing control, clock and track telephone subsystems, and a radio 
communication sub-system in five stations in the Cajueiro-Cabo section of the South Line; 

(n) construction of an additional facility in the Cavaleiro workshop for major 

overhauls of the EMU’s fleet, and of a small maintenance shed for the maintenance of 

diesel locomotives in the Cajueiro Seco station, and acquisition of miscellaneous 
equipment and spare parts for the rolling stock, and of maintenance equipment for the 
track and the catenary; and 

(o) general overhaul of about 25 EMUs and about 11 locomotives, rehabilitation 
and/or modernization of two diesel locomotives, installation of air conditioning systems 
in the EMU’s fleet, rehabilitation of about 21 passenger coaches, and acquisition of spare 
parts for the entire fleet.
Part B 

A. Design of an inspection and maintenance program for vehicle emissions and noise 
in the RMR. 

2. Preparation of a comprehensive program for traffic management and safety in the 
RMR, including an action plan for its implementation. . 

Part C 

l. Studies and action plans aimed at supporting the Transfer, including preparation 
of the institutional and organizational arrangements required therefor, valuation of assets 
to be transferred, transfer of personnel (including retirement and other benefits), and other 
legal and administrative aspects of the Transfer. 

2. (a) Studies and action plans to improve the management of the STU-REC System, 
including a manpower development plan to streamline the management and operational 
staff and to propose concise job descriptions, a study on cost accounting, management 
information systems and financial management, a plan to improve inventory management, 
a plan to subcontract maintenance and other operations to the private sector, a plan to 
increase non-operating revenues through the renting of station space, advertising and 
utilization of real estate. 

(b) Technical assistance and training to the STU-REC System’s and PE Entity’s 

staff as recommended by the manpower development plan referred to in paragraph (a) of 
this Part C.2. 

3. Studies and action plans to support the policy framework for implementing the 
transport modal integration in RMR, including: (a) a study on mechanisms for financing

SCHEDULE 4 

Special Account 

1. For the purposes of this Schedule: 

(a) the term "eligible Categories" means Categories (1), (2) and (3) set forth 
in the table in paragraph 1 of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; 

(b) the term "eligible expenditures" means expenditures in respect of the 
reasonable cost of goods and services required for the Project and to be financed out of 

the proceeds of the Loan allocated from time to time to the eligible Categories in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement; and 

(c) the term "Authorized Allocation" means an amount equivalent to 
$6,000,000 to be withdrawn from the Loan Account and deposited in the Special Account 
pursuant to paragraph 3 (a) of this Schedule, provided, however, that unless the Bank 
shall otherwise agree, the Authorized Allocation shall be limited to an amount equivalent 
to $3,000,000 until the aggregate amount of withdrawals from the Loan Account plus the 
total amount of all outstanding special commitments entered into by the Bank pursuant 
to Section 5.02 of the General Conditions shall be equal to or exceed the equivalent of . 

$20,000,000. | 

2. Payments out of the Special Account shall be made exclusively for eligible 
expenditures in accordance with the provisions of this Schedule. 

3. After the Bank has received evidence satisfactory to it that the Special Account 
has been duly opened, withdrawals of the Authorized Allocation and subsequent 
withdrawals to replenish the Special Account shall be made as follows: 

(a) For withdrawals of the Authorized Allocation, the Borrower shall furnish 
to the Bank a request or requests for a deposit or deposits which do not exceed the 
aggregate amount of the Authorized Allocation. On the basis of such request or requests, 
the Bank shall, on behalf of the Borrower, withdraw from the lLoan Account and deposit 
in the Special Account such amount or amounts as the Borrower shall have requested. 

(b) (1): For replenishment of the Special Account, the Borrower shall 
furnish to the Bank requests for deposits into the Special 
Account at such intervals as the Bank shall specify.
(ii) Prior to or at the time of each such request, the Borrower shall 

furnish to the Bank the documents and other evidence required 
pursuant to paragraph 4 of this Schedule for the payment or 
payments in respect of which replenishment is requested. On 
the basis of each such request, the Bank shall, on behalf of the 

Borrower, withdraw from the Loan Account and deposit into 
the Special Account such amount as the Borrower shall have 
requested and as shall have been shown by said documents and 
other evidence to have been paid out of the Special Account for 
eligible expenditures. 

All such deposits shall be withdrawn by the Bank from the Loan Account under 
the respective eligible Categories, and in the respective equivalent amounts, as shall have 
been justified by said documents and other evidence. 

4. For each payment made by the Borrower out of the Special Account, the 
Borrower shall, at such time as the Bank shall reasonably request, furnish to the Bank 

such documents and other evidence showing that such payment was made exclusively for 
eligible expenditures. 

5. Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraph 3 of this Schedule, the Bank shall 
not be required to make further deposits into the Special Account: 

(a) if, at any time, the Bank shall have determined that all further withdrawals 

should be made by the Borrower directly from the Loan Account in accordance with the 
provisions of Article V of the General Conditions and paragraph (a) of Section 2.02 of 
this Agreement; or 

(b) if the Borrower sinall have failed to furnish to the Bank, within the period 
of time specified in Section 4.01 (b) (ii) of this Agreement, any of the audit reports 
required to be furnished to the Bank pursuant to said Section in respect of the audit of the 
records and accounts for the Special Account; 

(c) if, at any time, the Bank shall have notified the Borrower of its intention 

to suspend in whole or in part the right of the Borrower to make withdrawals from the 
Loan Account pursuant to the provisions of Section 6.02 of the General Conditions; or 

(d) once the total unwithdrawn amount of the Loan allocated to the eligible 
Categories, less the amount of any outstanding special commitment entered into by the 
Bank pursuant to Section 5.02 of the General Conditions with respect to the Project, shall 
equal the equivalent of twice the amount of the Authorized Allocation.
Thereafter, withdrawal from the Loan Account of the remaining unwithdrawn 
amount of the Loan allocated to the eligible Categories shall follow such procedures as 
the Bank shall specify by notice to the Borrower. Such further withdrawals shall be made 

only after and to the extent that the Bank shall have been satisfied that all such amounts 
remaining on deposit in the Special Account as of the date of such notice will be utilized 
in making payments for eligible expenditures. 

6. (a) If the Bank shall have determined at any time that any payment out of the 
Special Account: (1) was made for an expenditure or in an amount not eligible pursuant 
to paragraph 2 of this Schedule; or (11) was not justified by the evidence furnished to the 
Bank, the Borrower shall, promptly upon notice from the Bank: (A) provide such 
additional evidence as the Bank may request; or (B) deposit into the Special Account (or, 
if the Bank shall so request, refund to the Bank) an amount equal to the amount of such 

payment or the portion thereof not so eligible or justified. Unless the Bank shall 
otherwise agree, no further deposit by the Bank into the Special Account shall be made 
until the Borrower has provided such evidence or made such deposit or refund, as the case 
may be. 

(b) If the Bank shall have determined at any time that any amount outstanding 
in the Special Account will not be required to cover further payments for eligible 
expenditures, the Borrower shall, promptly upon notice from the Bank, refund to the Bank 
such outstanding amount. 

(c) The Borrower may, upon notice to the Bank, refund to the Bank all or any 
portion of the funds on deposit in the-Special Account. 

(d) Refunds to the Bank made pursuant to paragraphs 6 (a), (b) and (c) of this 
Schedule shall be credited to the Loan Account for subsequent withdrawal or for 
cancellation in accordance with the relevant provisions of this Agreement, including the 

General Conditions.
