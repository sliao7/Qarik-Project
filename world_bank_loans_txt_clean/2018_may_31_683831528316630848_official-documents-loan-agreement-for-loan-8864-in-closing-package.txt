ARTICLE I - GENERAL CONDITIONS; DEFINITIONS

1.01. The General Conditions (as defined in the Appendix to this Agreement) apply
to and form part of this Agreement.

1.02. Unless the context requires otherwise, the capitalized terms used in this
Agreement have the meanings ascribed to them in the General Conditions or
in the Appendix to this Agreement.

ARTICLE H - LOAN

2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower the amount of five hundred million
United States dollars (USD 500,000,000), as such amount may be converted
from time to time through a Currency Conversion ("Loan"), to assist in
financing the project described in Schedule I to this Agreement (the "Project").

2.02. The Borrower may withdraw the proceeds of the Loan in accordance with
Section III of Schedule 2 to this Agreement.

2.03. The Front-end Fee is one quarter of one percent (0.25%) of the Loan amount.

2.04.' The Commitment Charge is one quarter of one percent (0.25%) per annum on
the Unwithdrawn Loan Balance.

2.05. The interest rate is the Reference Rate plus the Variable Spread or such rate as
may apply following a Conversion; subject to Section 3.02(e) of the General
Conditions.

2.06. The Payment Dates are June 15 and December 15 in each year.

2.07. The principal amount of the Loan shall be repaid in accordance with Schedule
4 to this Agreement.

2.08. (a) If on any given day, the Total Exposure exceeds the Standard Exposure
Limit (as said terms are defmed in sub-paragraphs (b)(ii) and (b)(iii)
of this Section), the Borrower shall pay to the Bank a surcharge at the
rate of one half of one percent (0.5%) per annum of the Allocated
Excess Exposure Amount (as defined in sub-paragraph (b)(i) of this
Section) for each said day ("Exposure Surcharge"). The Exposure
Surcharge (if any) shall be payable semi-annually in arrears on each
Payment Date.

(b) For purposes of this Section the following terms have the meanings set

forth below:

(i) "Allocated Excess Exposure Amount" means for each day

during which the Total Exposure exceeds the Standard

Exposure Limit, the product of: (A) the total amount of said

excess; and (B) the ratio of all (or, if the Bank so determines,

a portion) of the Loan to the aggregate amount of all (or the

equivalent portions) of the loans made by the Bank to the

Borrower, and to other borrowers guaranteed by the Borrower
that are also subject to an exposure surcharge, as said excess
and ratio are reasonably determined from time to time by the

Bank.

(ii) "Standard Exposure Limit" means the standard limit on the

Bank's financial exposure to the Borrower which, if exceeded,
would subject the Loan to the Exposure Surcharge, as
determined from time to time by the Bank.

(iii) "Total Exposure" means for any given day, the Bank's total

financial exposure to the Borrower, as reasonably determined

by the Bank.

SCHEDULE 1

Project Description

The objective of the Project is to strengthen the systems and processes of the
national PMGSY rural roads program for the expansion and maintenance of all-season
rural access roads, resulting in enhanced road connectivity to economic opportunities

and social services for beneficiary communities in the Participating States.

The Project consists of the following Parts:

Part A: PMGSY Rural Roads Program Financing

1. Green and Climate Resilient Rural Roads: Constructing, rehabilitating and/or
improving about 5,500 lan of priority rural roads and standalone bridges in
Selected Participating States as per the PMGSY Guidelines, incorporating

adequate climate resilience and road safety features to provide all-weather

access, of which 2,000 km incorporate green concepts and new technologies.

2. Pilot Projects to Introduce New Technologies: Piloting and demonstrating the

use of green and climate resilient road designs, innovative bridges and new

technologies, and retrofitting road safety and climate-resilient measures, in

about 1,500 kn of existing Through Routes and Link Routes identified in the

PMGSY District Rural Road Plans of Participating States (approx. 150-200 km

per state). -

3. Rural Roads under the Original Project: Completing construction of about

4,000 lan of new all-weather access roads to unconnected habitations in

Selected Participating States, including bridges and necessary cross drainage

works, financed under the Original Project, as per the PMGSY Guidelines.

Part B: Institutional Strengthening

1. Asset Management: (a) Developing and implementing state-level assets

management plans in Participating States to prepare prioritized plans for

capital investments and assets maintenance, including the development of a

road and bridge inventory and condition database; (b) refining PMGSY District

Rural Road Plans in Participating States, through identifying missing links and

unlinked habitations through the integrated planning of various categories of

roads; (c) developing innovative maintenance contracts for the rural roads core

network in Participating States, including the use of local communities and/or

micro-enterprise contractors; (d) providing technical assistance to study,

analyze and incorporate surface dressing and other innovative rural roads

maintenance technologies in Participating States; and (e) refining state-level

maintenance policies in Participating States.

2. Green & Climate Resilient Rural Roads Strategy: Developing a green and

climate-resilient rural roads strategy for PMGSY, incorporating green and

climate resilient concepts and supporting their implementation through state-
level strategy replication in Participating States, including: (a) the carrying out
of network level vulnerability assessments; (b) the revision of existing designs
and construction standards for rural roads and bridges; and (c) the development
of detailed engineering designs to pilot retrofitting climate-resilience in
existing rural roads and bridges in Participating States.

3. Skills Development and Gender-Targeted Opportunities: Refining PMGSY
training framework to develop a comprehensive human resources professional
development strategy for rural roads agencies and the construction industry
and supporting its implementation, including the creation of employment
opportunities for the youth and women self-help groups.

4. Road Safety Management: Developing state-level rural roads safety action
plans in Participating States, and supporting their implementation, through: (a)
the preparation of road safety engineering designs for retrofitting road safety
measures to the existing rural roads network (b) the strengthening of the
implementation capacity of, and the carrying out of awareness campaigns for,
local governments/agencies and communities on road and work-zone safety,
(c) the integration of road safety planning in the Participating States' asset
management systems; and (d) the carrying out of road safety audits of priority
rural roads.

5. Program Management Strengthening: Supporting system-wide improvements
for the PMGSY rural roads program, incorporating international best practices,
through: (a) the improvement of PMGSY documentation systems; (b) the
refinement and expansion of the OMMAS; (c) the refinement of PMSGY e-
procurement system and the Procurement & Contract Management Manual;
(d) the development of a medium term rural roads vision (strategy) document;
(e) the implementation of the recommendations of the PMGSY Assessment
Report; and (f) the carrying out of studies on further evolution of the PMGSY
rural roads program and/or the management of the rural road sector.

6. Research and Development: Establishing centers of excellence and an
accelerated testing facility for, and facilitating the participating of PMGSY's
technical agencies in carrying out: (a) research and development on rural roads
cost-effective designs, using local materials and new technologies; (b) capacity
building programs for rural road agencies; and (c) performance evaluations of
new rural roads technologies/innovations.

7. Outcome Monitoring- Carrying out outcome monitoring studies of PMGSY
roads including their contribution towards poverty reduction, employment,
income generation, health and education outcomes, and the achievement of the
Sustainable Development Goals, all disaggregated by gender.

8. Rural Transport Services and Agriculture Supply Chain: (a) Carrying out
studies on prospective cost-effective, reliable and safe improvements to rural
transport services through PMGSY rural roads network, catering to the needs
of women and the poor and promoting the use of clean fuel/electric vehicles;
and (b) carrying out studies on possible trade and logistics improvements in
rural areas to maximize the positive impact of rural roads on agricultural supply

chains.

9. Engineering Design, Project Management and Implementation: (a)

Developing cost-effective climate-resilient engineering designs for PMGSY

rural roads and associated surveys and research, (b) carrying out engineering
supervision of civil works and independent quality monitoring of PMGSY

rural roads in Participating States; (c) providing project management

(including techno-financial) support to NRRDA and the SRRDAs; (d) carrying

out performance auditing services for the Project; and (e) providing equipment

support for office communication, research and monitoring, planning and

design, IT tools/software, and quality assurance systems to NRRDA and the

SRRDAs of Participating States, for the carrying out of the PMGSY rural roads

program.

APPENDIX

Definitions

1. "Allocated Amount" means the amount allocated to each individual DLR, or
determined for each DLR pursuant to the respective formula set forth in the third
column of the table provided in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.

2. "Anti-Corruption Guidelines" means, for purposes of paragraph 5 of the Appendix
to the General Conditions, the "Guidelines on Preventing and Combating Fraud
and Corruption in Projects Financed by IBRD Loans and IDA Credits and Grants",
dated October 15, 2006 and revised in January 2011 and as of July 1, 2016.

3. "Bank's Safeguards Policies" means the Bank's operational policies and
procedures set forth in the Bank's Operational Manual under OP/BPs 4.01, 4.04,
4.36, 4.10,4.11, and 4.12, as published under www.WorldBank.org/opmanual.

4. "Category" means a category set forth in the table in Section I.A of Schedule 2
to this Agreement.

5. "Detailed Project Reports" means each of the detailed engineering reports prepared
or to be prepared by a Participating State and its SRRDA, in respect of any rural
roads and bridges to be constructed, rehabilitated, upgraded or retrofitted under the
PMGSY rural roads program, including detailed survey and investigations, design
and technology of choice, as well as social and environmental impacts and
mitigation measures therefor, all in accordance with the provisions of Chapter 6
(Project Preparation) of the Operations Manual.

6. "Disbursement-Linked Indicator" and the acronym "DLI" mean each of the
disbursement-linked indicators set forth in the first column of the table provided
in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.

7. "Disbursement-Linked Result" and the acronym "DLR" mean each of the
disbursement-linked targets/results set forth in the second column of the table
provided in Schedule 3 to this Agreement.

8. "District" means an administrative subdivision of a Participating State.

9. "DLR Period" means, in respect of any DLR, the period specified in the table
provided in Schedule 3 to this Agreement, during which such DLR is set to be
