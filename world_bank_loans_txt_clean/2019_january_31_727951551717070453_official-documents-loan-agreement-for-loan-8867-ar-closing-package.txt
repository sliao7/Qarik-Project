ARTICLE I - GENERAL CONDITIONS; DEFINITIONS

1.01. The General Conditions (as defined in the Appendix to this Agreement) apply to and form
part of this Agreement.

1.02. Unless the context requires otherwise, the capitalized terms used in this Agreement have
the meanings ascribed to them in the General Conditions or in the Appendix to this
Agreement.

ARTICLE II- LOAN

2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower the amount of one hundred and fifty million
Dollars (USD 150,000,000), as such amount may be converted from time to time through
a Currency Conversion ("Loan"), to assist in financing the project described in Schedule 1
to this Agreement ("Project").

2.02. The Borrower may withdraw the proceeds of the Loan in accordance with Section III of
Schedule 2 to this Agreement. The Borrower's Representatives for purposes of taking any
action required or permitted to be taken pursuant to this Section are its Minister of Treasury,
its Undersecretary of International Financial Relations, or its National Director of
Financing with International Credit Institutions.

2.03. The Front-end Fee is one quarter of one percent (0.25%) of the Loan amount.

2.04. The Commitment Charge is one quarter of one percent (0.25%) per annum on the
Unwithdrawn Loan Balance.

2.05. The interest rate is the Reference Rate plus the Variable Spread or such rate as may apply
following a Conversion; subject to Section 3.02 (e) of the General Conditions.

2.06. The Payment Dates are January 15 and July 15 in each year.

2.07. The principal amount of the Loan shall be repaid in accordance with Schedule 3 to this
Agreement.

ARTICLE III - PROJECT

3.01. The Borrower declares its commitment to the objective of the Project. To this end, the
Borrower shall carry out the Project, through GSA's DIPROSE:

(a) in coordination with GSA's Sub-secretariat of Political Coordination for Part A of
the Project, GSA's Secretariat of Agriculture, Livestock and Fisheries for technical

assistance activities under Part B of the Project, GSA's National Directorate of

Agricultural Emergencies and Disasters for Part C of the Project and the Selected
Public Institutions for Part A of the Project; and

(b) with the assistance of the Participating Provinces, under the terms of the
Framework Subsidiary Loan Agreements and Subsidiary Loan Agreements, for

Part B of the Project, all in accordance with the provisions of Article V of the

General Conditions and Schedule 2 to this Agreement.

ARTICLE IV - REMEDIES OF THE BANK

4.01. The Additional Event of Suspension consists of the following, namely that a Participating

Province has failed to comply with any of its obligations under its respective Framework

Subsidiary Loan Agreement or Subsidiary Loan Agreement.

4.02. The Additional Event of Acceleration consists of the following, namely, the event specified

in Section 4.01 of this Agreement occurs and is continuing for a period of 60 days after

notice of the event has been given by the Bank to the Borrower.

ARTICLE V - EFFECTIVENESS; TERMINATION

5.01. The Effectiveness Deadline is the date one hundred and twenty (120) days after the

Signature Date.

SCHEDULE 1

Project Description

The objective of the Project is to improve the management of agricultural risk by Eligible
Beneficiaries and Selected Public Institutions.

The Project consists of the following parts:

Part A. Strengthening Agroclimatic Information Systems

Support for improvements in the capacity and interoperability of agroclimatic and price information

systems operated and/or disseminated by GSA and other public and private entities through:

A.1. Development of Tools and Systems

Consolidation and integration of production, price, and meteorological information generated by

SMN, [NTA, CONAE, and Commodity Exchanges into information technology systems and

software solutions that improve access to agroindustrial information and enhance the assessment

of agricultural risks associated with climate variability, climate change, and market price

fluctuations.

A.2. Institutional Capacity Development

Capacity building in Selected Public Institutions and GSA to operate the information systems

improved and integrated under Part A. 1. of the Project and development of user interfaces to

facilitate the development of software solutions by the private sector, based on data generated by
such information systems.

Part B. Agricultural Risk Mitigation Investments

Support for the miti&ation of agricultural risk through:

B.1. Institutional Strengthening for the Integrated Management of Agricultural Risk

(a) Formulation of up to 15 Agricultural Value Chain Risk Strategies (AVCRSs) for
prioritized agricultural value chains and up to 15 Provincial Agricultural Sector
Risk Management Plans (PARMPs).

(b) Capacity building for national and provincial government institutions in the
integrated management of agricultural risks.

(c) Formulation of subprojects for climatic risk mitigation prioritized by PARMPs and

AVCRSs, and pre-investment studies for the design of other activities prioritized

in PARMPs and AVCRSs.
B.2. Public Investments

Carrying out of infrastructure, natural resource management or other weather risk management
investments (the Risk Mitigation Subprojects) identified and prioritized in: (a) AVCRSs; or (b)
PARMPs, in the latter case in a number of Participating Provinces not exceeding 12.

B.3. Adoption of Climate Smart Agriculture Technologies

(a) Training and technical assistance services for Vulnerable Family Farmers, Farmer
Groups, and Selected Financial Institutions to promote their adoption of validated
CSA technologies.

(b) Applied research, piloting, and dissemination of promising but untested CSA
technologies and risk management practices and instruments.

(c) Provision of Matching Grants to Eligible Beneficiaries to carry out on-farm or off-
farm investments required for the adoption and application of CSA technologies,
risk management practices, and instruments in their farming activities (the CSA
Technology Activities).

Part C. Agricultural Emergency Response Financing and Transfer of Agroclimatic Risks

Support for the improvement for the Borrower's agricultural emergency system at the national and
provincial levels and the expansion of risk coverage through:

(a) Training and technical assistance services to strengthen GSA's capacity to manage
agricultural emergencies.

(b) Development of agricultural risk financing instruments aimed to protect family
farmers with aggregated data at the provincial and regional-level such as
parametric insurance, catastrophe bonds, contingent lines of credit, and weather
derivatives, as well as training and technical assistance for their validation and
adoption.

(c) Pilot implementation of macro-level agricultural risk financing instruments to
protect family farmers against production risks in their farming operations.

Part D. Project Coordination, Monitoring and Evaluation

Support for:

(a) Project coordination and management, monitoring, results evaluation, and
assessment of Project activities;

(b) Project fiduciary administration, internal controls, and audits;
(c) Project environmental and social risk/safeguards management;

(d) citizen engagement mechanisms and grievance redress mechanism; and

(e) Project related studies proposed by GSA and agreed to by the Bank.
SCHEDULE 2

Project Execution

Section I. Implementation Arrangements

A. Institutional Arrangements

1. The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE, maintain for purpose of the
implementation, monitoring and supervision of the Project a structure, functions and
responsibilities, and staffing acceptable to the Bank, including fiduciary, administrative
and technical personnel, and social and environmental specialists, all with qualifications,
experience and terms of employment acceptable to the Bank, as set forth in the Operational
Manual.

2. The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE:

(a) not later than three months after the Signature Date start design of a digital registry,
operated and with a structure and mechanisms as set forth in the Operational
Manual, of beneficiaries of Part B of the Project; and

(b) as soon as technically and operationally possible but not later than nine months
after the Signature Date, start implementation of the digital registry of the
beneficiaries of all Parts of the Project.

B. Operational Manual

1 . The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE, carry out the Project in accordance with a
manual (the Operational Manual), acceptable to the Bank, which shall include, inter alia:

(a) the budgeting, accounting, auditing, reporting, financial, procurement and
disbursement procedures of the Project;

(b) the eligibility criteria for Vulnerable Family Farmers, Farmer Groups, Selected
Public Institutions, Selected Financial Institutions and Participating Provinces;

(c) the eligibility criteria and procedures for the selection, approval, carrying out,
monitoring and supervision of Risk Mitigation Subprojects and CSA Technology
Activities and related Matching Grants;

(d) the model form of Cooperation Agreement, Framework Subsidiary Loan
Agreement, Subsidiary Loan Agreement and Matching Grant Agreement;

(e) the organizational structure for implementation of the Project, including the
cooperating roles of the Selected Public Institutions;
(f) the Project indicators for the monitoring and evaluation of the Project; and

(g) the Safeguards Documents, including the grievance redress mechanism applicable
to the Project.

2. In case of any conflict between the terms of the Operational Manual and those of this
Agreement, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail.

C. Subsidiary Loan Agreement and Risk Mitigation Subprojects

1. The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE:

(a) prior to the implementation of any activity under Part B.2 of the Project for the
benefit of a Participating Province, enter into an agreement with the Participating
Province, under terms substantially similar to those in the Model Form of
Framework Subsidiary Loan Agreement and under terms and conditions
acceptable to the Bank, setting forth the framework for the implementation of Risk
Mitigation Subprojects in their jurisdiction; and

(b) prior to the implementation of any Risk Mitigation Subproject and not later than
24 months from the Signature Date, enter into a Subsidiary Loan Agreement with
the Participating Province under terms and conditions satisfactory to the Bank,
including the following terms:

(i) that the part of the proceeds of the Loan allocated for Part B.2 of the
Project and required for implementation of the Risk Mitigation Subproject
will be on-lent to the Participating Province as a subloan which shall be:
(A) denominated and repayable in Dollars; and (B) with financial terms
and conditions similar to those of the Loan; and

(ii) that the Borrower, through GSA's DIPROSE, require each Participating
Province to:

(1) carry out its Risk Mitigation Subproject with due diligence and
efficiency and in accordance with sound technical, economic,
financial, managerial, environmental and social standards and
practices satisfactory to the Bank, including: (A) the Participating
Province's undertaking to carry out its Risk Mitigation Subproject
in accordance with the provisions of the Safeguard Documents,
the Procurement Regulations, and the Anti-Corruption
Guidelines; (B) the Bank's and the Borrower's, through GSA's
DIPROSE, right to require audits of the Participating Province's
records and accounts; (C) the Borrower's, through GSA's
DIPROSE, right to suspend, terminate or accelerate or seek refund
of the subloan;
(II) (A) maintain a financial management system in accordance with
consistently applied accounting standards acceptable to the Bank,
in a manner adequate to reflect the operations, resources and
expenditures related to the Risk Mitigation Subproject; and (B)
enable the Bank's or the Borrower's representatives to have access
to such financial management system;

(I) enable the Borrower, through GSA's DIPROSE, and the Bank to
inspect the Risk Mitigation Subproject, its operation, including
environmental and social risk management, and any relevant
records and documents; and

(IV) prepare and furnish to GSA's DIPROSE, and the Bank all such
information as GSA's DIPROSE, or the Bank, shall reasonably
request relating to the foregoing.

2. The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE, exercise its rights under each Framework
Subsidiary Loan Agreement and Subsidiary Loan Agreement in such manner as to protect
the interests of the Borrower and the Bank and to accomplish the purposes of the Loan.
Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, the Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE,
not assign, amend, abrogate, terminate or waive any Framework Subsidiary Loan
Agreement or Subsidiary Loan Agreement or any of their provisions.

D. Cooperation Agreements

1 The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE:

(a) not later than four months after the Signature Date with respect to INTA, CONAE,
and SMN; and

(b) prior to initiating any activities for the strengthening of the agroclimatic and price
information systems operating in the Borrower's territory and involving the
cooperation of other Selected Public Institutions, enter into a cooperation
agreement with INTA, CONAE, SMN and the respective Selected Public
Institution, in terms substantially similar to those in the Model Form of
Cooperation Agreement and acceptable to the Bank, setting forth their respective
responsibilities in the implementation of such activities.

2. The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE, exercise its rights under each Cooperation
Agreement in such manner as to protect the interests of the Borrower and the Bank and to
accomplish the purposes of the Loan. Except as the Bank shall otherwise agree, the
Borrower, through GSA's DIPROSE, shall not assign, amend, abrogate, terminate or waive
any Cooperation Agreement or any of their provisions.
E. CSA Technology Activities and Matching Grants

1. The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE, provide Matching Grants to Eligible
Beneficiaries for the implementation of CSA Technology Activities under Part B. 3 (c) of
the Project in accordance with eligibility criteria and procedures satisfactory to the Bank
and specified in the Operational Manual.

2. Prior to the initiation of any CSA Technology Activity by an Eligible Beneficiary, the
Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE:

(a) enter into an agreement with the Eligible Beneficiary, in terms substantially similar
to those in the model form of Matching Grant Agreement, and in terms and
conditions acceptable to the Bank; and

(b) exercise its rights and carry out its obligations under each Matching Grant
Agreement in such manner as to protect the interest of the Borrower and the Bank
and to accomplish the objectives of the Project. Except as the Bank shall otherwise

agree, the Borrower shall not assign, amend, abrogate, terminate of waive or fail
to enforce any Matching Grant Agreement or any of its provisions.

F. Safeguards

The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE:

(a) implement and cause the Participating Provinces to implement, the Project in

accordance with the ESMF, RPF and IPPF;

(b) comply and cause the Participating Provinces to comply, with the procedures
detailed in said ESMF, RPF and IPPF for environmental screening and social
assessment, evaluation, implementation and monitoring of the Project, including
the procedures for the preparation of ESMPs, RAPs and IPPs, if applicable; and

(c) as applicable, implement and cause to be implemented by the Participating
Provinces and Eligible Beneficiaries, the pertinent ESMPs, RAPs and IPPs, all in

accordance with their terms and in a manner acceptable to the Bank.

Section II. Proiect Monitoring Reporting and Evaluation

The Borrower shall, through GSA's DIPROSE, furnish to the Bank each Project Report not later

than 45 days after the end of each calendar semester, covering the calendar semester.
