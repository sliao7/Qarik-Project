SCHEDULE 1 Project Description The objective of the Project is to increase the safety of students, teachers, and staff in selected schools in high-risk seismic zones in Turkey. The Project consists of the following parts: Improving Seismic Resilience of Schools (a) (b) (c) (d) Reconstruction of Schools. (i) Reconstruction of selected schools in high seismic risk provinces in the Borrower’s territory, using design standards acceptable to the Bank that relate to, inter alia, accessibility, flexibility, adaptability, learning, and environmentally friendly materials. (ii) Provision of modern equipment to complement the learning environment. Retrofitting of Schools. Retrofitting for seismic strengthening of selected schools in high seismic risk provinces in the Borrower’s territory. Seismic Risk Assessments. Conducting of additional seismic risk assessments, including development or refinement of technical specifications to be used, land analysis, geotechnical surveys, design and design review, and supervision of construction works. Communications. Carrying out of a communications campaign to publicize to the broader school communities the long-term benefits of said retrofitting and reconstruction activities, and school-based disaster risks. Enhancing Institutional and Technical Capacity for Safer Schools Provision of technical assistance to enhance the engineering and analytical capacity of the Construction and Real Estate Department (“CRED”), including: (a) (b) (c) development of additional modules or layers of information for MoNE’s geographic information system on hazard information and risk assessment, and prioritization of investment planning; provision of structural performance analysis of MoNE’s school designs before and after retrofitting to determine the added value of planned interventions; preparation of cost-benefit analysis on retrofitting and reconstruction for various school typologies, and analysis of alternative retrofitting methods for specific school designs;Part 3. (d) (e) (f) (g) (h) preparation of damage and loss estimation and development of typical recovery and repair designs based on selected scenarios for various school typologies; improvement of the minimum design criteria for school buildings, and development of standards and regulations to prevent non-structural damages; carrying out of climate vulnerability assessment to identify risks and adaption measures; carrying out of research on innovative technologies to improve efficiency of new constructions and retrofits; and enhancement and further development of CRED’s capacity to detect, monitor and address Project implementation requirements, including, inter alia, quality assurance, occupational health and safety, and site-level monitoring. Project Management Provision of goods, consulting services, non-consulting services, and Operating Costs to build capacity and support CRED in carrying out the Project and in Project management, including in the areas of procurement, financial management, disbursement, environmental and social safeguards compliance, and monitoring and evaluation, including data collection, and implementation assessments.SCHEDULE 2 Project Execution Section I.
