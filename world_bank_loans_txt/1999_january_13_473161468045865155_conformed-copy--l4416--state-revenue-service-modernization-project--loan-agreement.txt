SCHEDULE 2

Description of the Project

The objective of the Project is to support the implementation of measures 

for: (a) developing the revenue system and assisting in its evolution into a system 
which would foster voluntary compliance, reduce tax evasion and, thus, enable 
reduction of tax rates; and (b) upgrading the capacity of the State Revenue Service 
(SRS) for effective and transparent administration of tax and social insurance laws 
and regulations.

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 

thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:

Part A:

Organization and Management of SRS

Provision of consultants’ services, training and equipment for the 

implementation of measures to increase the capacity and efficiency of SRS, including: 
(1) development of an organizational structure for SRS; (2) development and 
implementation within SRS of a methodology for planning, budgeting and financial and 
asset management; (3) improvement of administrative processes for administration of 
tax, customs and social insurance laws and regulations, and making them, whenever 
relevant, compatible with  the requirements of the European Union; (4) modernizing 
SRS’ personnel rules and enhancing the capacity of its Training Unit; (5) development 
of internal audit strategy and of guidelines for dealing with cases of corruption, and 
establishment of a unit (the Vigilance Unit) to promote transparent administration of 
tax and custom regulations; (6) design and implementation of public relations campaign 
to increase awareness of the equity of taxation and to encourage voluntary compliance 
with and transparent administ
ration of tax rules; and (7) upgrading SRS’ management information system.

Part B:

Operational Improvements

Provision of consultants’ services, training and equipment to assist in the 

implementation of measures to improve certain operations of SRS, including (1) 
tax-payer registration; (2) tax declaration, collection and recovery of arrears; (3) 
audit and investigation; (4) customs operations and procedures, including 
anti-smuggling activities; (5) adjudication and appeal of tax decisions; (6) 
tax-payers and client services; and (7) common information technology.

The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 2002.
