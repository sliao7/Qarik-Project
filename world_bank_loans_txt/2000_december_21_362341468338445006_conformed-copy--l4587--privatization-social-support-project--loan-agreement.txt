SCHEDULE 2

Description of the Project

The objectives of the Project are to support the achievement of the 

objectives of the Borrower’s Privatization Program, help to mitigate the 
negative social and economic impact of the privatization of state-owned 
enterprises by the Borrower, and monitor the social impact of the 
Economic Reform Program.

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such 

modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from 
time to time to achieve such objectives:

Part A:

Job Loss Compensation

1.
Provision of job-loss compensation payments to workers of 
state-owned enterprises displaced by labor restructuring as a result of 
the privatization of such state-owned enterprises.

Provision of technical assistance to develop and maintain 

2.
administrative framework to enable the delivery of job-loss compensation 
payments.

Part B:

Labor Redeployment

Provision of technical assistance and goods to develop and maintain 

1.
administrative framework to enable the delivery of redeployment services 
to workers displaced as a result of the privatization of state-owned 
enterprises.

Provision of labor redeployment services, including job counseling, 

2.
temporary community employment, retraining programs, small business 
assistance, incubator services and other related services, through 
Service Providers, to workers displaced as a result of the privatization 
of state-owned enterprises, including secondary layoffs.

Part C:

Social Impact of Economic Reform Program

Provision of technical assistance and goods for: (a) the evaluation 

of the impact of the Economic Reform Program; (b) the analysis of social 
issues and identification of policy alternatives for social assistance; 
and (c) the assessment of the social impact on workers displaced by 
privatization and the evaluation of the net impact of labor redeployment 
services. 

Part D:

Project Management

Provision of support to the PCU and LAG in Project coordination and 

implementation, including the provision of equipment, technical 
assistance and training. 

The Project is expected to be completed by June 30, 2004.
