The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives: Part_A: Financial Sector Regulation Provision of coordinated technical assistance through SHCP with respect to: 1. CNBV (a) Banks and Financial Groups: Analysis and improvement of: authorization policies and procedures; regulatory and supervision policies; regulatory accounting principles; supervision processes (both in situ and extra situ) and consolidated supervision of financial conglomerates; modernization of the information systems; and institutionalization of human resource development. (b) Securities Markets and Brokerage Firms: Analysis and improvement of: the regulatory and supervisory policy framework of the organized securities markets arid brokerage firms; self-regulation; and the legal, regulatory and supervision policy framework for new financial instruments such as, for example, derivatives. (c) Support for the execution of the Program: Support for the execution of the Program including, inter alia, technical assistance with respect to the Guarantor’s accelerated program of bank inspections, diagnostic studies of banks, selection of advisors for bank restructurings, a study of appraisal standards, review of impediments to securitization of bank and non-bank originated assets, the UDI-scheme, a study of the legal framework for secured transactions, and a study of the obstacles to voluntary debt restructuring (including recognition, execution and enforcement of out-of-court workouts and arbitration awards).          2. CNSF Analysis and improvement of: supervision processes; regulatory and supervisory policies applicable to insurance companies, insurance brokers, adjusters and reinsurance companies; CNSF’s capacity to evaluate actuarial audits; certification procedures for actuaries; and regulatory reporting standards and accounting principles consistent with those adopted in the other Commissions. 3. CONSAR (a) Regulatory Framework: The establishment of a regulatory framework for the private investment management of SAR accounts, the development of a consistent regulatory framework for voluntary occupational retirement plans offered by private. sector and _parastatal enterprises, the development of CONSAR’s capacity to supervise and inspect the SAR accounts, the design of a public communications program in support of pension reform, and preparation of a human resource development program. (b) Annuities Market Study: Execution of a study by CONSAR, jointly with CNSF, of the annuities market and its implications for its further development as the SAR matures, with emphasis on international experience. Part B: Public Investment Evaluation and Budgeting Provision of technical assistance to SHCP’s unit responsible for public investmenit evaluation and budgeting with respect to: inter-institutional coordination; establishment of evaluation standards, dissemination of these standards and training in project evaluation; evaluation of sector- and region-specific public investment projects; establishment of a national inventory of public investment projects and a system for ranking and prioritization within and across economic sectors; multi-annual budgeting; and ex-post monitoring and evaluation.          -9.
