The Project consists of the following parts: Component 1: Network Improvement Strengthening pavement, upgrading and widening of approximately 500 km of high priority state highway corridors of the Core Road Network, including: (a) providing road safety enhancement features; (f) ensuring proper and timely road maintenance for at least a five (5) year period post construction; and (g) setting up a web-based and integrated project management information system. Component 2: Institutional Strengthening of PWD Strengthening the institutional capabilities of PWD in order to strengthen its asset management practices and the planning and prioritization of investments using scientific approaches, through: (a) the operationalization of a road asset management system integrate with a geographic information system database; (b) the strengthening of the quality management system, developing a comprehensive quality management policy, testing protocols, institutional arrangements and allocation of responsibilities therefor; and (c) the provision of training to PWD staff on, inter alia, contract management, and social and environmental safeguards. Component 3: Road Safety (a) Road Safety Initiative by the HD: Establishing the UPHP to implement proactive, highly visible, general deterrence of trauma-promoting offences and enforcement of traffic regulation, in at least two (2) high-risk highway corridors, including: (i) social marketing of the UPHP mandate and operation; (ii) monitoring and evaluation of road safety performance within the selected corridors; (iii) state-wide implementation of a road crash database and analysis system; and (iv) training on, inter alia, first-aid, trauma care, crash attendance as first responders, and traffic laws enforcement, including international exposure visits and knowledge exchange. i | (b) Road Safety Initiatives by the TD: Strengthening the institutional capacity of the TD as a nodal agency for road safety within Uttar Pradesh, by: (i) providing technical assistance to the RSC for the carrying out of road safety management, the development of the road safety strategy and action plans consistent with the State Road Safety Policy; (ii) carrying out state-wide media campaigns to promote the State Roads Safety Policy; (iii) strengthening the commercial driver training and certification including upgradation of a driver training institute on training aspects; (iv) developing optimal designs for vehicle testing stations and establishing up to five (5) such stations; (v)         eae SERIE PERO ATENEO SNES: READIED NOTES LEADER SARA AEA TER EY SUE    exploring potential private sector engagement in vehicles inspection and testing; (vi) training TD’s staff on road safety management, data analysis, and the preparation, implementation and monitoring of a road safety strategy and action plans, including international exposure visits and knowledge exchange. (c) Road Safety Initiatives by the PWD: Improving the safety of state highway network and strengthening the institutional capacity of PWD in roads safety by: (i) designing, implementing and evaluating a state-wide program of cost-effective road safety engineering measures on the Core Road Network; (ii) carrying out research and evaluating the effectiveness of safety engineering improvements in the roads network; and (iii) strengthening the capabilities of the RSD through the provision of technical assistance and staff training, including international exposure visits and knowledge exchange, in order to mainstream road safety into the road design, operation and maintenance practices of PWD.
