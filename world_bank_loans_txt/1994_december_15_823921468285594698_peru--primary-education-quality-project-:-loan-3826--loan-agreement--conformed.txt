The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives:   Part A: Educational Quality Improvement 1. Design of an adjusted curriculum, for single teacher schools and for third grade through sixth grade in other public primary schools, with improved content linkages between and within grades and more flexible, diversified and responsive to the cultural and social diversity of Peru. 2. A program: (a) to provide textbooks, workbooks and related didactic guides for first through sixth grades in all public primary schools; (b) to provide a collection of supplementary reading materials and non-printed didactic materials, including learning games, puzzles, alphavet blocks and posters, to all public primary schools; and (c) to design and implement a strategy to achieve excellence in the delivery of primary education, including, inter alia, the establishment of model educational centers. 3. A training program for: (a) primary school teachers to strengthen their competencies in student-centered instructional methods and classroom management; and (b) primary school principals in pedagogical supervision methods. 4, Design and operation of a voucher system to finance in-service training for primary school teachers. Part B: Modernization of Educational Administration 1, (a) A management audit study to evaluate the necessary structural and procedural changes and capacity-building actions required to improve the management of the educational system; and (b) a program to improve the management by MOE of the educational system.        -19- 2. (a) The Pilot Plan for Modernization of School Management, including its monitoring and initial and final evaluation; and (b) a program of gradual decentralization for the education sector. 3. The establishment and operation of: (a) a management information system to assist MOE in its planning, monitoring and decision-making functions; and (b) an information system to facilitate the exchange of information and learning experiences of schools. 4. (a) The establishment and operation of a student assessment system; (b) the strengthening of the capabilities of MOE in student assessment through workshops, short- term visiting courses and training for MOE’s technical committee on student assessment; and (c) a program to strengthen the capacity and information on student assessment at the national level through promotional activities and the provision of research grants and graduate training on a competitive basis. Part C: Infrastructure Improvement 1. (a) The construction of approximately 3,200 primary school classrooms in the Targeted Schools; (b) the rehabilitation of approximately 960 primary school classrooms in the Targeted Schools; and (c) the improvement of sanitation services and reconditioning of primary school classrooms and office space in the Targeted Schools. 2. A program for school maintenance consisting of: (a) a computerized information system including, inter alia, a data base, training and other procedures for school rehabilitation and maintenance; (b) the updating and publication of approximately 3,000 manuals with technical specifications for school buildings and classroom furniture; and (c) the design, production and distribution of approximately 25,000 school maintenance manuals. | 3. (a) Fumishing of all primary school classrooms constructed under the Project and approximately 380 primary school classrooms rehabilitated under the Project; (b) the provision of office furniture to approximately 490 Targeted Schools with primary school classrooms constructed under the Project; (c) the provision of bookshelves to all primary school classrooms for storage of the textbooks, non-printed didactic materials and supplementary reading materials distributed under the Project; and (d) the provision of teaching equipment for the model educational centers to be established under Part A 2(c) of the Project.      - 20 - 4, (a) The establishment and operation of a computerized monitoring system for school mapping and infrastructure; and (b) the update and computerization of the national cadastre of educational infrastructure.
