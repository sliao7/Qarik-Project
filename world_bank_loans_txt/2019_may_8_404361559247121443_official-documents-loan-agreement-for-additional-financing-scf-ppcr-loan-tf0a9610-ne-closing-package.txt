The Project consists of the Original Project as modified below: Component 1: Mainstreaming Climate Resilience into Development Strategies at the 1.1. 1.2. National and Local Levels Mainstreaming Climate Resilience. Providing technical assistance, goods and services to make the social and economic development policies in the health, water and road infrastructure sectors more responsive to climate change, through: (a) (b) (c) at the central level: (i) the establishment of a multidisciplinary task force to ensure a participatory policy formulation process; (ii) the carrying out of in-depth studies and assessments in order to identify potential climate scenarios over the next decade; (iii) the establishment of major tools for planning, monitoring and evaluation of climate-sensitive investments; and (iv) the organization of large public consultations; and at the local level: (i) the review, revision in order to incorporate climate sensitive issues, and validation of the guidelines for local development planning in collaboration with the DGAT/DC; (ii) the provision of support to the Targeted Communes in the preparation or revision of their local development plans and their annual budgets in order to better integrate climate-sensitive initiatives; and (iii) the provision of institutional support to the Borrower’s National Agency for the Investments of Local Municipalities (Agence Nationale pour l’Investissement des Collectivités Territoriales). the preparation of detailed action plans based on national sector strategies and local developmental plans prepared under Parts 1.1(a) and 1.1(b) of the Project or any other relevant national programs and plans, including the preparation of feasibility studies, analytical studies and other assessments that would inform the future implementation of these action plans. Communication Strategy and Knowledge Management. Providing technical assistance, goods and services for the purpose of defining and implementing a comprehensive communication strategy and a system of effective knowledge management, including the organization of general sensitization, social1.3 mobilization and information initiatives, as well as specialized Training sessions addressed to key stakeholders. Integration of New Technologies to the Knowledge Management System. Providing technical assistance, goods and services for the purpose of integration of new technologies to the knowledge management system developed under sub- component 1.2, such as satellite imagery and other state-of-the art technologies, to help inform national and regional decision-making, including on spatial planning and disaster-risk management, as well as to help in the monitoring of the Project’s impact. Component 2: Integrating Climate Resilience Practices into Agro-Sylvo-Pastoral 2.1. 22: Systems and Local Populations’ Social Protection Measures Providing to Targeted Communes technical assistance and funding for the financing by Targeted Communes of technical assistance, works, goods and services to improve resilience of agro-sylvo-pastoral systems and local populations to climate variability and change, including by: Improving the resilience of agro-sylvo-pastoral systems, by: (a) scaling up sustainable land and water management practices to reduce climate change impacts through capacity building activities in the areas of agriculture, agro-forestry, agro- pastoralism and pastoralism, selected with a gender-sensitive approach, and with the aim of improving agricultural productivity, sustainable management of forest resources, and productivity of grazing areas; and (b) providing Matching Grants to Targeted Communes to finance activities to improve agricultural productivity, sustainable management of forest resources and productivity of grazing areas, included in Targeted Communes’ approved local development plans and budgets. Integrating innovative measures to protect the poorest and most vulnerable households, by: (a) designing and establishing a system aimed at integrating into local planning innovative measures for the protection of the poorest and most vulnerable households through flexible social protection actions, including actions listed in paragraph (b) immediately below; and (b) providing Matching Grants to Targeted Communes to finance social protection actions as part of the implementation of the system designed and established in paragraph (a) immediately above, including: (i) the protection and rehabilitation of socio-economic community facilities that are vulnerable to main effects of climate risks; (ii) the implementation of a simple, realistic and well performing cash transfer system for the most vulnerable households; (iii) the organization of remunerated seasonal labor intensive activities for the members of the poor households; and (iv)the distribution of food stamps to chronically poor households in order to buy essential food items, each on the basis of targeting, payment or distribution, monitoring and evaluation, and grievance systems acceptable to the World Bank. Establishing and operationalizing integrated multi-services platforms at Targeted Communes (“Maisons du Paysan”) by financing works, technical assistance, Training and equipment, including the provision of advisory services offered by Maisons du Paysan to local communities on topics aimed at improving the performance of family farms and agricultural enterprises, such as sustainable land management practices, storage and trade of food, fodder supplements and agricultural inputs, knowledge dissemination, use of new technology for climate forecasting and access to data; and gender inclusiveness and sensitivity. Component 3: Ensuring Coordination of all Activities of the Project (including monitoring and evaluation activities) and SPCR Overall Strategic Coordination Ensuring coordination of the Project activities, including monitoring and evaluation. Ensuring strategic coordination of the SPCR and knowledge sharing concerning the Project’s and SPCR’s approach, results, challenges and impacts at the national level with key stakeholders and at the international level with other countries participating in the Pilot Program for Climate Resilience.Section I.
