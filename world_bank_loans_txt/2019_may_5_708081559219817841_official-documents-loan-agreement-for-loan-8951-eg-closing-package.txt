ARTICLE II — LOAN The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower the amount of two hundred million United States Dollars (USD 200,000,000), as such amount may be converted from time to time through a Currency Conversion (“Loan”), to assist in financing the project described in Schedule 1 to this Agreement (“Project”). The Borrower may withdraw the proceeds of the Loan in accordance with Section III of Schedule 2 to this Agreement. The Borrower’s representative for purposes of taking any action required or permitted to be taken pursuant to this Section is the Micro Small and Medium Enterprises Development Agency. The Front-end Fee is one quarter of one percent (0.25%) of the Loan amount. The Commitment Charge is one quarter of one percent (0.25%) per annum on the Unwithdrawn Loan Balance. The interest payable by the Borrower on the principle amount of the Loan withdrawn and the amount outstanding from time to time for each Interest Period shall be at a rate equal to the Reference Rate (as defined in the Appendix to the General Conditions) for the Loan Currency plus the Variable Spread; provided, that upon a Conversion of all or any portion of the principle amount of the Loan, the interest payable by the Borrower during the Conversion Period on such amount shall be determined in accordance with the relevant provisions of Article IV of the General Conditions.2.06 2.07. 2.08. 2.09. 3.01. 4.01. 4.02. 5.01. -2- The interest rate is the Reference Rate plus the Variable Spread or such rate as may apply following a Conversion; subject to Section 3.02(e) of the General Conditions. The Payment Dates are May 15 and November 15 in each year. The principal amount of the Loan shall be repaid in accordance with Schedule 3 to this Agreement. The Borrower represents that it has designated its Ministry of Finance for the purpose of handling, on behalf of the Borrower, debt service payments with respect to the Loan. The Project consists of the following parts: Part 1. Financial Support to MSMEs Provision of Financial Intermediary Loans (“FI Loans”) to Participating Financial Intermediaries (“PFIs”) for the purpose of on-lending as Sub-loans to Eligible Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises (“Eligible MSMEs”) to carry out job creating Sub- projects in the territory of the Borrower. Part 2. Risk Capital for Innovative Startups and High Growth SMEs Provision of Equity and Quasi Equity Investments to Eligible Risk Capital Intermediaries that target innovative startups and high growth SMEs (“Eligible RCIs”) in the territory of the Borrower; and associated Management Costs. Part 3. Business and Capacity Development A. (i) Provision of Training, mentoring, coaching and business development services to Eligible MSMEs, PFIs and Eligible RCIs for Parts 1 and 2 of the Project; (ii) provision of technical assistance to enhance MSMEDA’s usage of digital solutions, including for a digital matching platform aimed at connecting entrepreneurs with potential investors; and (iii) developing a robust monitoring framework, including for job creation under the Project. B. Supporting Project management activities, monitoring and evaluation and strengthening the capacity of MSMEDA, to coordinate and execute project activities, through the provision of technical assistance, Training, preparation of relevant reports, Operating Costs and required goods for the purpose.6 SCHEDULE 2 Project Execution Section I. Implementation Arrangements Institutional Arrangements The Borrower shall cause MSMEDA to be responsible for the implementation of the Project and shall take all actions, including the provision of funding, personnel and other resources necessary, to enable MSMEDA to perform its functions. To facilitate the day-to-day implementation of Project activities, the Borrower shall cause MSMEDA to maintain at all times during the implementation of the Project, a Project Implementation Unit (“PIU”) to run the day-to-day management of the Project, in form and with terms of reference, functions, composition, mandate, staffing, and adequate resources satisfactory to the Bank, and as further described in the Project Operational Manual. In order to carry out Part 2 of the Project, the Borrower shall cause MSMEDA to establish and maintain throughout the life of the Project, an independent Investment Committee, with composition, mandate and terms of reference, satisfactory to the Bank and as further detailed in the Project Operational Manual. The Borrower shall, through MSMEDA, ensure that the Investment Committee is responsible for monitoring the implementation of Part 2 of the Project, including, inter alia: (a) selecting and monitoring the Equity and Quasi Equity Investments and the Eligible RCIs; (b) adopting investment policies and guidelines applicable to Part 2 of the Project; (c) approving MSMEDA’s exits from Eligible RCIs in accordance with the respective Shareholder Agreement and as further detailed in the Project Operational Manual. Project Operational Manual The Borrower shall, through MSMEDA, prepare and adopt, no later than (1) one month after the Effective Date, a Project Operational Manual in form and substance satisfactory to the Bank. Said Manual shall include provisions on the following matters: (a) arrangements on financial management, setting forth the detailed policies and procedures for financial management under the Project; (b) procurement management procedures; (c) institutional administration, coordination, and day-to-day execution of Project activities, including the respective roles and responsibilities of MSMEDA, Investment Committee and any other entity involved in Project implementation; (d) monitoring and-7- evaluation; (e) reporting; (f) information and communication of Project activities; (g) guidelines for assessing potential environmental and social impacts of the Project and designing appropriate mitigation, management, and monitoring measures in respect of such impacts; (h) all arrangements for the implementation of Part 1, Part 2 and Part 3 of the Project, including FI loans, Sub-loans, Sub- projects, Equity and Quasi Equity Investments criteria and pricing; and (i) such other technical and organizational arrangements and procedures as shall be required for the Project. The Borrower shall, through MSMEDA, afford the Bank a reasonable opportunity to exchange views on said Project operational manual, and thereafter, adopt such Project Operational manual as shall have been approved by the Bank (“Project Operational Manual” or “POM”). The Borrower shall, through MSMEDA, carry out the Project in accordance with the POM. To this end, the POM shall not be amended, abrogated or waived, without the prior written approval of the Bank. In case of any conflict between the provisions of the POM and the provisions of this Agreement or the Project Agreement, the provisions of this Agreement or the Project Agreement shall prevail.
