The objectives of the Project are to assist the Borrower in carrying out its long-term plan aimed at the sustainable develcpment of its infrastructure sector, including: (a) assessing strategic priorities for reducing infrastructure bottlenecks; (b) developing an updated strategy for public and private partnership and suitable frameworks for private participation in the provision of infrastructure services; (c) redefining the relative roles cf central and local authorities in the implementation of infrastructure projects; and (ad) enhancing program evaluation capability in central and regional planning bodies, and implementation capabilities in line agencies and local governments. 2. The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives: Part A: Sub-projects 1. Carrying out of Sub-projects designed to strengthen the Borrower’s capacities for: (a) designing and implementing infrastructure development activities, through the provision of advisory services to prepare: (i) sector policies and planning frameworks; (ii) strategies to develop institutions and increase public and private partnership in the provision of infrastructure services; and (iii) feasibility studies, engineering designs, envi- ronmental and resettlement assessments and remedial plans for proposed activities, inter alia, in the following sub-sectors: power generation and distribution, port, toll roads, water supply and environmental sanitation development and management, water resources management, transport and communications; and (b) assessing policy and strategic studies and private investment proposals, through the review of said studies and proposals by panels of independent experts.        -12- Part B: Strengthening Implementation Capacity Provision of training and equipment for the staff of the PMU and PIUs, to strengthen their capacity for the selection, monitoring and evaluation of consultants’ services and for project management and privatization and negotiation techniques.
