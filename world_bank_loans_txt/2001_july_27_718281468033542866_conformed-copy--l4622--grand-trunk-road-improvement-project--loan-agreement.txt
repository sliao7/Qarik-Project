SCHEDULE 2

Description of the Project

The objectives of the Project are: (a) to reduce transport constraints on 

national economic activity; and (b) to improve institutional capabilities to manage 
road programs, assets and services on a commercial basis.

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications 

thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives:

Part A:

Highway Upgrading

Widening and strengthening about 420 km of national highways from 2 lanes to 4-6 

1.
lane divided carriage-ways, with service roads, bypasses and other traffic separation 
measures where required.

Carrying out a program of resettlement and rehabilitation of people affected by 

2.
the carrying out of Part A.1 of the Project and implementing measures to mitigate 
adverse environmental impacts of the carrying out of Part A.1 of the Project.

Part B:

Road Safety and Corridor Management Works

1.
accidents and improving safety on the national highway network.

Carrying out a program of road improvements specifically targeted at reducing 

2.
methods and strengthen maintenance management practices on the national highways.

Carrying out a program of maintenance works to introduce alternative contracting 

Part C:

Public-private Partnership in Road Concession

Undertaking detailed preparation and implementation of a pilot project to 

upgrade a selected section of national highways under a public-private partnership 
scheme.

Part D:

Institutional Strengthening

Provision of technical assistance to NHAI: (a) for building NHAI’s social and 
environmental management capacity, including a poverty impact of highway development 
study and consultant services for training and streamlining monitoring procedures; (b) 
for training trainers and disseminating road safety manuals; (c) for conducting public 
road safety education and campaigns through mass media; (d) for implementing a 

comprehensive road safety action plan; (e) for computerizing the national highway 
assets inventory system; (f) for strengthening its Corridor Management Units; (g) for 
conducting a cross-state border road transport efficiency study; and (h) for 
conducting sector-related studies and pre-investment studies for the preparation of 
possible future projects, including a possible expressway project.

The Project is expected to be completed by June 30, 2006.
