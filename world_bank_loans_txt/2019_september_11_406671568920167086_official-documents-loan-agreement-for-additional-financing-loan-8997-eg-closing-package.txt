The Project consists of the Original Project, as modified below: Part A: Provision of Conditional and Unconditional Cash Transfers Supporting the provision of conditional and unconditional Cash Transfers to Beneficiaries under the Borrower’s Takaful and Karama Cash Transfer Program. Part B: Support of Social Safety Nets Targeting and Operational Systems 1. Enhancing the targeting and operational systems of the Takaful and Karama Cash Transfer Program, through, inter alia: (i) improving the systems and business process of said program, including for case management, payment delivery and communications} (ii) improving the administrative infrastructure and operational support of relevant units under the MOSS; (iii) providing Training for relevant MOSS staff and other relevant officials of the Borrower to carry out the Takaful and Karama Cash Transfer Program; (iv) monitoring of the Takaful and Karama Cash Transfer Program, including strengthening the Grievance Redress Mechanism and citizen engagement activities; and (v) reassessment of eligibility criteria for Beneficiaries as well as assessment and recertification processes of new applicants. 2. Expanding the Karama Program, through: (i) roll out of a national Karama disability plan; (ii) institutional capacity building to develop enhancement disability assessment tools and registration data; (ili) provision of Training to selected medical professionals; and (iv) linking Karama Program’s grievance redress mechanism to the TKP’s MIS. Part C: Project Management and Monitoring and Evaluation   Supporting Project management and implementation activities, through: (1) carrying out of Project monitoring, regular internal audits, annual external audits and annual independent verification of the Cash Transfers; (2) maintaining the PIU and supporting the operational and implementation capacity of the PIU staff; and (3) undertaking independent targeting, process and impact evaluations of the Project.    linking Beneficiaries to employment services, provision of Training, and Asset transfers to said Beneficiaries.    -7- SCHEDULE 2 Project Execution Section I. Implementation Arrangements Institutional Arrangements The Borrower shall cause MOSS to maintain, throughout the implementation of the Project, the PIU, CASP and PTF, with mandate, functions and resources, terms of reference, and with staff of adequate qualifications and experience, all acceptable to the Bank, to be responsible for overall implementation and coordination of Project activities and such other functions as set forth in the Project Operations Manual. Without limitation on the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Section, the Borrower shall cause MOSS to maintain the PIU to include, throughout the implementation period of the project, key staff to carry out the Project, including but not limited to a project director, a financial management specialist, a procurement specialist, a monitoring and evaluation specialist a senior environmental and _ social specialist, an environmental officer and a social officer, all under terms of reference and experience acceptable to the Bank. The Borrower shall carry out oversight and coordination of the implementation of the Project through the Ministerial Committee for Social Justice. Project Operations Manual The Borrower shall, no later than six (6) months after the Effective Date, cause the MOSS to update and adopt the Project Operations Manual in a manner satisfactory to the Bank and thereafter implement the Project in accordance with its provisions. Except as the Bank may agree after due consultation, the Borrower shall ensure that MOSS shall not amend, abrogate or waive any provision of the Project Operations Manual without the prior written agreement of the Bank. In the case of any conflict between the arrangements and procedures set out in the Project Operations Manual and the provisions of this Agreement, the provisions of this Agreement shall prevail. Selection of Beneficiaries Without limitation on the provisions of Section I.B of this Schedule, the Borrower shall ensure that the selection of Beneficiaries for purposes of Part A of-8- the Project shall be undertaken in a manner and in accordance with eligibility criteria and procedures as set out in the Project Operations Manual. Safeguards The Borrower, through MOSS, shall ensure that the Project is carried out in accordance with the provisions of the ESMF, and to that end, if any activity included in Project would, pursuant to the ESMF, require the adoption of an ESMP: (a) (i) prepare such ESMP and furnish it to the Bank for review and approval in accordance with the ESMF; and (ii) thereafter disclose such ESMP as required by the ESMF and approved by the Bank prior to implementation of the activity in question; (iii) thereafter implement the ESMP during the Project implementation; and (iv) incorporate said ESMP in the bidding documents; and (b) thereafter take such measures as shall be necessary or appropriate to ensure compliance with the requirements of such ESMP. The Borrower shall, through MOSS, ensure that all institutional capacity building activities, including consultants’ services under the Project, is only undertaken pursuant to terms of reference reviewed and found satisfactory by the Bank, such terms of reference to ensure that the technical assistance takes into account, and calls for application of the Bank’s environmental and social safeguards policies and the Borrower’s own laws relating to the environment and social aspects. The Borrower shall, through MOSS, ensure that employees, agents, service providers, contractors and subcontractors carry out the Project in conformity with acceptable environmental and social standards, practices and codes of conduct (which shall, inter alia, contain measures that prohibit, endeavour to prevent and address Project-related gender based violence and sexual exploitation and abuse), in accordance with the provisions of the Borrower’s environmental and social laws. The Borrower shall, through MOSS, ensure that relevant environmental and social mitigation measures and clauses are included in the tender documents in accordance with the Safeguard Instruments, in addition to ensuring that contractors’ EMPs/ESMPs are approved by the Bank prior to commencement of works subject to environmental and social assessment. Without limitation upon its other reporting obligations under this Agreement, the Borrower shall through MOSS regularly collect, compile and submit to the Bank every calendar semester, reports, in form and substance satisfactory to the Bank, on the status of compliance with the Safeguard Instruments, giving details of:-9- (a) measures taken in furtherance of such Safeguard Instruments; (b) conditions, if any, which interfere or threaten to interfere with the smooth implementation of such Safeguard Instruments; and (c) remedial measures taken or required to be taken to address such conditions. The Borrower shall, throughout Project implementation, maintain and publicize the availability of Project-level grievance, feedback and redress mechanism, in form and substance satisfactory to the Bank, to hear and determine fairly and in good faith all complaints and feedback raised in relation to the Project, and take all measures necessary to implement the determinations made by said grievance feedback and redress mechanism in a manner satisfactory to the Bank. The Borrower shall not amend, abrogate, repeal, suspend, waive, or otherwise fail to enforce, or permit to be amended, abrogated, repealed, suspended or waived any of the Safeguard Instruments or any provision thereof without the prior written approval of the Bank. In case of inconsistency between this Agreement and any of the Safeguard Instruments, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail. Other Implementation Arrangements The Borrower shall, through MOSS, no later than three (3) months after the Effective Date, hire an independent Verification Agent under terms of reference, acceptable to the Bank, to carry out annual Cash Transfer performance audits. The Borrower shall ensure that reports of said performance audits of the Takaful and Karama Cash Transfers Program are furnished to the Bank not later than forty-five (45) days after the end of each Fiscal Year during the implementation of the Project. The Borrower shall, through MOSS, hire in a timely manner an independent entity under terms of reference acceptable to the Bank: (a) to undertake an end- line impact evaluation for the Project; and (b) to conduct rapid assessment, process evaluation and impact evaluation of the Economic Inclusion and Empowerment Services Pilot. The Borrower shall through MOSS, update the Grievance Redress Mechanism to handle complaints and grievances from Beneficiaries or third parties relating to any aspects of the Project, including allegations of fraud and corruption. Such mechanism shall, inter alia, contain procedures for recording of complaints and grievances, directing the complainants to the appropriate level for action, the-10- review process, and provision of feedback to the complainant on the action taken on the basis of best practice service standards.
