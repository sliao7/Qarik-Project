Part A: Municipal Solid Waste Separation, Collection, Sorting and Transportation Strengthening the waste management system in the Project Areas, infer alia, through: (1) (ii) (iil) (iv) Part B: residential waste separation through, inter alia, the provision of separation bins and bags; public places waste separation, through the provision of bins for recyclable and non-recyclable waste, and recyclable material collection structures: waste storage. transfer and sorting through: (a) the construction of about four medium to large and two small transfer stations (including a sorting facility for recyclable materials within one transfer station); and (b) conversion of about 22 smaller transfer stations for parking space for smaller vehicles. rest areas for sanitation workers staff, or temporary storage space for compression equipment and bulk municipal waste; and waste collection and transportation through the provision of vehicles for produce market and household kitchen food waste, and bulk waste transfer trucks. Kitchen Waste Treatment Construction of a treatment plant in Yinzhou district to process kitchen waste from households and markets. Part C: Project Implementation Support Provision of technical advisory services and support for the introduction, inter alia, of: (1) (ii) (111) (iv) a citywide program for public and community mobilization, education, and awareness-raising to encourage waste separation throughout the Project period; Output-based Incentive Payments to neighborhood residents committees to encourage waste separation; Output-based Incentive Payments for restaurants that install and operate oil-water separators: rules and regulations for solid waste scparation: a4.{v) a municipal solid waste pricing policy; (vi) assessment of bio-chemical characteristics of domestic solid waste and new treatment technologies; and (vii) asolid waste information management system. Part D: Capacity Building and Project Management Support Provision of technical advisory services for: (1) design review, advisory services, construction supervision, Project management and monitoring; (ii) training in waste minimization and municipal solid waste management for staff at neighborhood, sub- district, district, and municipal levels; and (iii) external monitoring and evaluation of performance in waste separation.      SCHEDULE 2 Project Execution Section I.
