Within this context the Project has three principal objectives, namely: (a) to increase the supply of well-trained, market-oriented practitioners through implementing of: (i) development Sub-projects to prepare the content, materials and instructors for training activities; and (ii) delivery Sub-projects to provide training for priority target groups, e.g. enterprise managers, accountants, bankers, government officials, etc.; (b) to develop the NIF as a new mechanism of financing training programs; (c) to mobilize additional resources, channel project funds for priority market-oriented training programs on a competitive basis and to make external aid flows move more effectively and efficiently through NTF; and (d) to establish the basis for a broader second- phase project by piloting effective interventions on other key market areas. The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives: Part As Management Development Provision of training, fellowships, consulting services, training equipment and materials for supporting developments in the following areas: (1) Policy framework and quality standards for management development; (2)  Enterprise-based management development; (3) Learning materials, trainers and teachers; (4) Management consulting; and (5) Networking and information.        -17- Part B: Financial Sector Training (1) Training Materials Development, testing and publishing of training materials in accounting and finance, including case studies, advance text-books with teaching aids, and computer-based self-training courses. (2) Building Training Infrastructure Upgrading selected existing training facilities and establishment of up to a total of eight information technology centers in the three regions, Moscow, St.Petersburg and Nizhny Novgorod. (3) Training Practitioners and Executives Carrying out of mid-term and short-term training programs and conferences in accounting and finance for practitioners and senior executives. (4) Training Faculty Development of teaching faculty in accounting, auditing and corporate finance. (5) Developing Curricula Designing and development of an up-to-date national accounting and finance curricula at the university level. (6) Training of Commercial Bankers Provision of training in bank accounting and commercial bank operations and management. Part Cs: Public Finance Training (1) Training in budget and taxation policy. (2) Training of Treasury officials in budget execution.          - 18 - Part D: Pilot Projects Carrying out of Sub-projects on the pilot basis and preparation studies in other areas of market-oriented training, not listed in Parts A, B and C.
