SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project — 

The objectives of the Project are: (a) to improve the quality of Basic Education 
in the territory of the Borrower; (b) to increase student enrollment in Basic Education, 

including the enrollment of children from low-income families; and (c) to strengthen the 
management capacity of SEEBAC in respect of Basic Education. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof 
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives: 

Part A: Educational Development 

1. Curriculum Reform 

(a) Carrying out of a national campaign to promote the introduction of a new 
curriculum for Basic Education. 

(b) Provision of technical assistance to SEEBAC in order to assist SEEBAC 
in introducing the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above in their respective 
public schools which provide Basic Education, including the provision of training required 
therefor. 

(c) Design and distribution of manuals for teachers and Parents’ Associations 
to provide teachers and Parents’ Associations in the area of Basic Education with 
information on the contents of the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above and 

to assist SEEBAC in monitoring the introduction of such curriculum in public schools 
providing Basic Education, including the provision of training required therefor. 

(d) Creation of additional curriculum resource centers in SEEBAC’s regional 
offices in order to assist public schools providing Basic Education in adopting the new 
curriculum referred to in Part A.1 (a) above, including the provision of training to such 
centers’ personnel and the acquisition and utilization of the equipment required therefor. 

(e) Carrying out of Curriculum Innovations. 

(f) Design and distribution of guidelines for SEEBAC to manage the 
implementation of the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above. 

  

 



  

  

  

-17- 

(g) Carrying out of a review on the progress made in introducing the new 
curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above in public schools providing Basic Education. 

2. Educational Materials 

(a) Design, production and distribution of textbooks and workbooks (which 
will be designed based on the new curriculum referred to in Part A.1(a) above) in the 
areas of mathematics, natural sciences, social sciences and Spanish for students of public 

schools attending first through eighth grades. 

(b) Design and distribution of teachers’ guides that will enable the teachers 
to use the textbooks and workbooks referred to in Part A.2(a) above. 

(c) Acquisition and distribution of required educational materials (other than 
textbooks and workbooks referred to in Part A.2(a) above) under the new curriculum 
referred to in Part A.1(a) above for students attending public schools providing Basic 
Education. 

(d) Acquisition and distribution of classroom didactic packages (each package 
consisting, inter alia, of a map, a globe and educational charts) for all public schools 
providing Basic Education. 

(e) Acquisition and distribution of school supplies consisting, inter alia, of 
notebooks, pencils and erasers for low-income students attending public schools providing 
Basic Education, in accordance with the criteria set forth in the Operational Manual. 

(f) Creation of school libraries for the benefit of pre-school classes and first 
through eighth grades. 

(g) Creation of an educational materials entity that will coordinate and 
manage: (i) the design, production, printing or acquisition (as the case may be) and 
distribution of textbooks, workbooks and other educational materials to public schools 
providing Basic Education and (ii) the sale of textbooks, workbooks and other educational 
materials to private schools providing Basic Education, including the provision of 
technical assistance and the acquisition and utilization of the equipment required therefor. 

3. Human Resources Development

Section I. Procurement of Goods and Works 

Part A: General 

Goods and works shall be procured in accordance with the provisions of Section 
I of the "Guidelines for Procurement under IBRD Loans and IDA Credits" published by 
the Bank in January 1995 (the Guidelines) and the following provisions of this Section, 
as applicable. 

Part B: International Competitive Bidding 

1. Except as otherwise provided in Part C of this Section, goods and works shall be 
procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of Section II of the 
Guidelines and paragraph 5 of Appendix 1 thereto. 

2. The following provisions shall apply to goods and works to be procured under 
contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 1 of this Part B: 

(a) Grouping of contracts 

To the extent practicable, contracts for goods and works shall be grouped in bid 
packages estimated to cost each $250,000 equivalent or more and $3,000,000 or more 

each, respectively. * 

(b) Two-stage bidding procedure 

The bidding procedure for library books shall be carried out in two stages in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraph 2.6 of the Guidelines. 

(c) Preference for domestically manufactured goods | 

The provisions of paragraphs 2.54 and 2.55 of the Guidelines and Appendix 2 
thereto shall apply to goods manufactured in the territory of the Borrower.

Part C: Other Procurement Procedures 

1. Limited International Bidding 

Pharmaceuticals, computer and peripheral equipment, software and vehicles which 
the Bank agrees can only be purchased from a limited number of suppliers, regardless of 
the cost thereof, may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the 
provisions of paragraph 3.2 of the Guidelines. 

2. National Competitive Bidding 

(a) Educational Materials estimated to cost more than $50,000 but less than 
$250,000 equivalent per contract, up to an aggregate amount equivalent to $2,000,000, 
may be procured under contracts awarded in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 
3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines. 

(b) Works estimated to cost more than $250,000 equivalent but less than 

$3,000,000 equivalent per contract, may be procured under contracts awarded in 
accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 3.3 and 3.4 of the Guidelines. 

(c) In the procurement of Educational Materials and works in accordance with 

this Part C.2, the Borrower shall use standard bidding documents acceptable to the Bank. 

3. National Shopping 

Educational Materials estimated to cost $50,000 or less per contract, up to an 

aggregate amount equivalent to $1,800,000, may be procured under contracts awarded on 
the basis of national shopping procedures in accordance with the provisions of paragraphs 
3.5 and 3.6 of the Guidelines. 

4, Procurement of Small Works 

Works estimated to cost $250,000 or less per contract, and not to exceed 

$3,000,000 equivalent in the aggregate may be procured under lumpsum, fixed price 
contracts awarded on the basis of quotations obtained from three qualified domestic 
contractors in response to a written invitation. The invitation shall include a detailed 
description of the works, including basic specifications, the required completion date, a 
basic form of agreement acceptable to the Bank, and relevant drawings, where applicable. 
The award shall be made to the contractor who offers the lowest price quotation for the 

required work, and whe has the experience and resources to successfully complete the 
contract.
