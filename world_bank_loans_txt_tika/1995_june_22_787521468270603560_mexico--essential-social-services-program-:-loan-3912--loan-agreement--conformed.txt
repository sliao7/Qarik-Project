SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objectives of the Project are to: (a) protect essential social services for the 
poor in Mexico in the areas of education and health care; (b) strengthen existing social 
safety net measures, through support for retraining, short-term employment and nutrition 

programs, to cushion the impact on the poor in Mexico of the country’s current economic 
situation; and (c) support the establishment of monitoring mechanisms to lay the 
foundation for measuring and achieving efficiency gains in the provision of services in 
the social sectors in Mexico. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof 
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve the above- 
mentioned objectives: 

Part A: ’ Basic Education Services 

1. Provision of public education services to the poor in preschool (ages 4 and 5) and 
primary (grades 1-6) and lower secondary (grades 7-9) levels through programs 
administered by CONAFE. 

2. (a) Provision of free textbooks through CONALITEG to schoolchildren in the 
preschool and primary levels, as well as provision through CONALITEG of printed 
teaching guides and other complementary publications for use by teachers at such levels; 
and (b) carrying out of a study on the cost-effectiveness of CONALITEG’s textbook 
production and distribution system. 

3. Construction through CAPFCE of educational spaces (including classrooms and 
support facilities) for public preschools serving the poor, of educational spaces (including 
classrooms, workshops and support facilities) for lower secondary public schools served 
by SEP’s teaching-by-television program (telesecundaria) and of educational spaces for 
boarding schools serving indigenous students. 

Part B: Basic Health Services 

1. Provision to the poor in Mexico who lack health insurance of priority preventive 
health services (such as vaccination of children, control of endemic and communicable 

diseases, screening for early detection of chronic diseases, family planning and 

reproductive health services, maternal and child health care and health and nutrition 
education), together with support for the social marketing of public health programs. 

  

 



  

  

~14- 

2. Provision to the poor in Mexico who lack health insurance of basic curative health 
care through basic health care (first level health centers and second level hospitals) and 
mobile outreach units. 

3. Construction, equipping and maintenance of public first and second level health 

facilities and public health laboratories. 

Part C: Training and Employment 

1. Provision of vocational classroom, in-plant and on-the-job basic skills training, 
as well as tool kits, through PROBECAT to otherwise unemployed or underemployed 
workers, together with provision of stipends to such trainees during the course of their 

training. 

2. Carrying out of public works projects urider the PEE. 

Part D: Nutrition 

Provision of a basic food basket (consisting of corn flour, beans, salt, sugar and 

cooking oil), micronutrient supplements, growth monitoring services and nutrition 
education to pregnant or lactating women and children under age 5 living in selected 
impoverished municipalities. 

Part E: Monitoring and Evaluation 

1. Provision of technical assistance, equipment and supplies to establish a system for 
monitoring the outputs generated by social sector expenditures (so as to allow budgeting 

decisions for programs in such sectors to be made on the basis of the effectiveness of 
prior expenditures for such programs). 

2. Carrying out of physical audits and qualitative assessments of beneficiaries as part 
of the monitoring of social sector outputs. 

* * * 

The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 1996.
