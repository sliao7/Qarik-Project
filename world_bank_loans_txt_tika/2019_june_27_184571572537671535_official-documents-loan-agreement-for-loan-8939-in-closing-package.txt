SCHEDULE 1 

Project Description 

The objective of the Project is to build capacity for better management of State Highways 
and to improve traffic flows on Selected State Highways in the State of Rajasthan. 

The Project consists of the following parts (“Components”): 

‘Component 1: Operationalization of the Rajasthan State Highways Authority 

Supporting the institutional development and operationalization of RSHA through: (a) 
establishing its organizational and governance structure; (b) setting up RSHA’s human 
resource management, financing and financial management, procurement, contract 
management, risk management and accountability systems and frameworks, including 
environmental and social safeguard management functions; and (c) establishing and 

operationalizing the RSHA Fund as per the RSH Act. 

Component 2: State Highways Improvements 

(a) 

(b) 

(c) 

(d) 

Upgrading and Maintenance through EPC Contracts: Widening, upgrading and improving 
approximately 472 km of existing State Highways and Major District Roads to two 
lane/four lane roads, through EPC Contracts with five-year maintenance requirements 
post-construction, including: (i) realignment of road formation, provision of 
granular/paved shoulders, provision of structures and strengthening of pavement; (ii) 
design and deployment of ducting; (iii) safety improvement measures; and (iv) construction 
of toll plazas. 

Supervision of EPC Contracts: Retaining the services of one or more Authority 
Engineer(s). 

Upgrading and Maintenance through PPP/HAM Contracts: Widening, upgrading and 
improving approximately 294 km of existing State Highways and Major District Roads to 
two lane/four lane roads, through PPP/HAM Contracts contemplating their maintenance 
during the ten-year concession period, including: (i) realignment of road formation, 
provision of granular/paved shoulders, provision of structures and strengthening of 
pavement; (ii) design and deployment of ducting and/or laying of fiber optic cables along 
formation; (iii) safety improvement measures; (iv) construction of toll plazas; and (v) 

piloting and demonstrating digital highways concept in approximately 110 km of State 
Highways, through the design and implementation of intelligent transports systems, 
including automated toll management systems and control centers, incident detection and 
management systems, variable message signs, speed management systems with license . 

plate recognition, speed cameras, and solar-powered animal-crossing warning systems. 

Supervision of PPP/HAM Contracts: Retaining the services of one or more Independent 
Engineer(s).

Road Asset Management System (“RAMS”) and Network Planning Tool: (i) Finalizing and 

implementing the RAMS in PWD and RSHA,; (ii) developing a linked system for future 
highway network expansion/upgrading, and corresponding RSHA Investment Plans; and 
(iii) strengthening the existing asset management cells in PWD and RSHA, including the 

provision of training, communications and knowledge exchanges with other states and/or 
countries. 

Citizen Engagement and Accountability Mechanisms. Designing and implementing: (i) a 
comprehensive communications strategy for Project beneficiaries and Affected Persons; 

and (ii) a Grievance Redress Mechanism. 

Capacity Building of PWD’s and RSHA’s Staff: Providing training and building that 
capacity of PWD and RSHA on, inter alia, procurement and financial management, public- 
private partnership on highways, contract management, design of green and digital 

highways, social and environmental safeguards, road safety and corporate governance, 
including attending international and/or domestic courses, study tours, workshops, 
international exposure visit, and other knowledge exchanges.

standard operating procedures; and (iii) implementation support to relevant stakeholders in

Streamlining and Piloting Speed Management: Piloting a speed management program 
along selected high-risk corridors, including: (i) undertaking studies on current speed

enforcement activities; (ii) identification of approximately 100 km of high-risk corridors 
in State Highways and Major District Roads for piloting speed management measures.

surveys on approximately 500 km of high-risk State Highways/Major District Roads to 

establish their road safety levels.

“Affected Person” means persons who, on account of the execution of the Project, have 

experienced or would experience direct economic and social impacts caused by: (a) the 
involuntary taking of land, resulting in: (i) relocation or loss of shelter; (ii) loss of assets or

persons must move to another location; or (b) the involuntary restriction of access to legally 
designated parks and protected areas, resulting in adverse impact on the livelihoods of such 
persons. 

“Allocated Amounts” means the amount allocated to each individual DLR, or determined 

for each DLR pursuant to the respective formula, set forth in the third column of the table 
provided in Schedule 3 to this Agreement. 

‘Anti-Corruption Guidelines” means, for purposes of paragraph 5 of the Appendix to the 
General Conditions, the “Guidelines on Preventing and Combating Fraud and Corruption 
in Projects Financed by IBRD Loans and IDA Credits and Grants”, dated October 15, 2006
