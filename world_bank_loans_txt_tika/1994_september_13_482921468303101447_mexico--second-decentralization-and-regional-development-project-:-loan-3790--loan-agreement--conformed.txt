ARTICLE II 

The Loan 

Section 2.U1. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the 
terms and conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement, 
various currencies that shall have an aggregate value equivalent to 
the amount of five hundred million dollars ($500,000,000), being the 

sum of withdrawals of the proceeds of the Loan, with each withdrawal 
valued by the Bank as of the date of such withdrawal. 

Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from 

the Loan Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to 
this Agreement for expenditures made (or, if the Bank shall so 
agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable cost of goods, works 
and services required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to 
this Agreement and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan. 

(b) The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, 
open and maintain in dollars a special deposit account in Banco de 
México on terms and conditions satisfactory to the Bank. Deposits 
into, and payments out of, the Special Account shall be made in 
accordance with the provisions of Schedule 4 to this Agreement. 

Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be June 30, 1999 or such 
later date as the Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly 
notify the Borrower and the Guarantor of such later date. 

Section 2.04. The Borrower shall pay to the Bank a commitment

time.

Manual.

SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objective of the Project is to alleviate poverty in the 
Project States by increasing access of rural poor and indigenous 
communities to basic social and economic infrastructure and to 
income generating activities. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such 
modifications thereof as the Borrower, the Guarantor and the Bank 

may agree upon from time to time to achieve such objectives: 

Part A: Rural Poverty Alleviation and Community Participation 

Support of SEDESOL’s poverty alleviation efforts in the 
Project States through: 

l. Municipal investment and institutional development 

(a) Execution of small-scale investments under SEDESOL’s 
Municipal Funds Program. 

(b) Execution of a program of technical assistance, 

training, acquisition of appropriate equipment, and studies to 
strengthen rural and semi-rural municipalities and creation and 
operation of the ITGs and MPWGs for fiscal strengthening and for the 
Municipal Funds Program. 

2. Rural Water Supply 

(a) Rehabilitation, construction and extension of water 

supply systems in localities with not more than 5,000 inhabitants 
nor less than 500 inhabitants as defined by the 1990 INEGI census, 
except as otherwise agreed with the Bank. 

(b) Institutional strengthening of the State Water Agencies 

and local water organizations in the Project States, including 
creation and operation of the ITG and MSU for rural water supply. 

3. Rural Roads Rehabilitation and Maintenance 

(a) Rehabilitation of Selected Rural Roads through spot 
improvements.   
 



  

  

  

-~17- 

(b) Routine maintenance of rural roads eligible under the 
Project Operational Manual through the use of contracted lengthmen 
(peones camineros). 

(c) Institutional strengthening of the State Road Agencies, 
including creation and operation of the ITG and the MSU for rural 
roads. 

4. Income Generating Programs 

Support of Income Generating Programs targeted to poor 
regions, including indigenous groups and women’s groups in those 
regions, in the Project States under a plan approved by the Bank. 

Part B: Institutional Strengthening of SEDESOL 

l. Execution of a program of technical assistance, studies, 
training and acquisition of appropriate equipment for the support of 
SEDESOL’s execution of Part A of the Project, including the 
provision of technical assistance and consultants to support the PCU 

and to carry out annual financial audits and physical performance 
evaluations. 

2. Execution of such technical assistance and studies to further 

the objective of the Project as may be approved by the Bank.
