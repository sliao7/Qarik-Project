ARTICLE 0 

The Loan 

Section 2.01. The Bank agrees to lend to the Borrower, on the terms and 
conditions set forth or referred to in the Loan Agreement, various currencies that shall 
have an aggregate value equivalent to the amount of twenty-five million Dollars 

($25,000,000), being the sum of withdrawals of the proceeds of the Loan, with each 

withdrawal valued by the Bank as of the date of such withdrawal. 

Section 2.02. (a) The amount of the Loan may be withdrawn from the Loan 
Account in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 1 to this Agreement for 
expenditures made (or, if the Bank shall so agree, to be made) in respect of the reasonable 
cost of goods and services required for the Project described in Schedule 2 to this 
Agreement and to be financed out of the proceeds of the Loan. 

(b) The Borrower shall, for the purposes of the Project, open and maintain 
in Dollars a special deposit account in Banco Nacional de Panama or other commercial 
bank acceptable to the Bank, on terms and conditions satisfactory to the Bank, including 
appropriate protection against set-off, seizure or attachment. Deposits into, and payments 

out of, the Special Account shall be made in accordance with the provisions of Schedule 
5 to this Agreement. 

Section 2.03. The Closing Date shall be June 30, 2000 or such later date as the 
Bank shall establish. The Bank shall promptly notify the Borrower of such later date.

Semester.

SCHEDULE 2 

Description of the Project 

The objectives of the Project are: (a) to reduce the incidence of malnutrition 
among children aged less than 5 years, breast-feeding and pregnant women; (b) to 
increase the coverage of basic water supply and sanitation in the Borrower’s rural areas; 
(c) to train JAAs so as to improve their capacity to operate and maintain systems under 
their responsibility; and (d) to strengthen MDS capacity to plan, execute, monitor and 
evaluate: (i) the policies, strategies and technical standards developed for the Borrower’s 
rural health sector; and (ii) rural water quality. 

The Project consists of the following parts, subject to such modifications thereof 
as the Borrower and the Bank may agree upon from time to time to achieve such 
objectives: 

Part A: Nutrition 

1. Training and equipping of approximately 1,500 PSs and approximately 120 ASs 
that will provide primary health care services to approximately 70,000 rural inhabitants; 
such training to include monitoring of the nutritional status, and treatment and prevention 
of malnutrition, mainly in rural areas. 

2. Introduction and operation of a food supplement program targeted on 
malnourished members of the following vulnerable groups: (a) children aged more than 
6 months and less than 60; and (b) pregnant and breast-feeding women. 

3. Carrying out of a refresher training program for MDS’ staff assigned to the 
regional and local offices, on the following subjects: 

(a) identification, monitoring, treatment and prevention of nutritional 

problems in the community, at the primary and referral levels of the health system, 
including effective community organization and interpersonal communication 
methodologies; 

(b) supervision of ASs, PSs and community organizations, including health 
committees; and 

(c) methodologies for fostering organization and promotion of community and 

families’ small food production and storage development projects. 

  
   



  

- 26 - 

Part B: Rural Water Supply and Sanitation 

1. (a) Design, construction and supervision of approximately: 

(i) 450 new rural water supply systems; 

(ii) 300 drilled wells and 150 dug wells, all equipped with 
handpumps; and 

(iii) 30,000 latrines. 

(b) Renovation or expansion of approximately 250 existing water supply 
systems in rural communities. 

2. (a) Establishment or regularization of JAAs. 

(b) Implementation of a system in MDS to supervise and assist JAAs in the 
operation and maintenance of basic water supply and sanitation infrastructure, including: 

(i) training of MDS’s sanitary inspectors and health educators in 
methodologies for promoting effective community participation 
in the selection and construction of basic water supply and 

sanitation systems; 

(ii) training of community leaders in the organization and operation 

of JAAs; and 

(iii) training and technica! assistance for designated JAA members. 

3. Establishment of the Borrower’s national system of water quality controls in rural 
areas, including: 

(a) establishment and operation of a central water quality laboratory in MDS 
and basic biological and physico-chemical water analysis facilities in four regional offices; 
and 

(b) creation of a computerized information center on the Borrower’s water 
sources for rural areas and related sector activities.        



  

  
Part C: Institutional Strengthening 

1. Strengthening the capacity of SDGSP to direct, monitor and evaluate the nutrition 
strategies and programs to be implemented under the Project. 

2. Strengthening of SDGSA aimed at allowing MDS to perform its statutory 
obligations with regard to: 

(a) providing households living in small rural communities with access to 
basic water supply and sanitation infrastructure; and 

(b) defining and enforcing environmental health regulations to protect the 
general public from water-borne contamination or pollution. 

3. Operation of UAP. 

4, Design and implementation, within MIPPE, of a national system for monitoring 
poverty alleviation efforts within the social sectors. 

* * * 

The Project is expected to be completed by December 31, 1999.
